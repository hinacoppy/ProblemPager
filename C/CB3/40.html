Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 52?
pips Red=154 White=144
In a money game and with Red owning the cube 10/8, 6/1 would be necessary. Everything else loses too many gammons.

At DMP things are very different as gammons donâ€™t count and Red needs to make that move that maximises his game winning chances. To that end he needs to free his rear checkers and 23/21 must be part of the move. 23/16 is just too wide , even at DMP. 23/21, 13/8 gives up the mid-point too prematurely.

The correct play is 23/21, 10/5 which seeks to establish the 21-pt and at least safeties the checker on the 10-pt. Even after this play Red will win only 27% of the time but that is better than with any other play.
