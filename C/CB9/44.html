The one thing you cannot do is what Red did over the board and that is play 7/3, 7/2 hoping to escape his rear checker later.

The position demands action and therefore 8/4* is more or less forced. The question then is whether to risk the double slot 8/3 or play more quietly with 6/1.

The former seeks to build a perfect home board as soon as possible (assuming White fans) while the latter seeks to gain a tempo to escape the rear checker with less immediate risk.

Rollouts have this as a virtual dead heat with the double slot winning by 0.006. The key is to make sure you hit with 8/3* and then understand why both plays of the 5 are reasonable. Estimating that both plays would have virtually identical equities would be beyond anybody over the board where you have to make judgements based on principles and experience. Tricky game, backgammon.
