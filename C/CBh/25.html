Players often misjudge positions like this and I have seen people drop redoubles in similar positions. If you think your opponent might drop then by all means redouble but in truth Red has work to do before he can redouble.

He must also know enough to execute a trap play with small rolls such as 31 (7/6, 7/4) and 32 (7/5, 7/4) but not with rolls such as 54 or 53 (they take the checkers too deep into the home board). He can use the cube to greater effect later once White has (hopefully) split his rear checkers by rolling a single six.

Red is a big favourite in this position (73%) but there is virtually no volatility so he should hold the cube here to see how the position develops and then hopefully give White a difficult take/drop decision in due course. In the actual position Whiteâ€™s take is trivial.
