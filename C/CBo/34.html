Prime versus prime once again but of course Red is very much in the driving seat.

A case could be made for 7/1(2) to make sure White can never make the second anchor but giving up the full prime before escaping the rear checker is a tactical error. !3/7(2) also looks reasonable but in fact 13/10, 13/4 is more flexible.

Best of all is 13/1. Red doesn’t mind being hit and if he is hit that might help to destroy White’s timing. If not hit he can proceed with escaping his rear checker and perhaps get hit next turn. He can make the ace-point in his own good time.

This variation is powerful enough to make any move other than 13/1 an error and 7/1(2) is very nearly a blunder.
