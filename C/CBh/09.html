Money Play. How should Red play 55?
pips Red=139 White=125
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
In the old days I would have played 18/13(2), 18/8 but that takes all the pressure off White’s two stripped outer points.

Playing 13/3(2) creates a very strong broken prime while maintaining the pressure on those two points while Red still has a spare checker (on the 18-pt) to play with.

Broken primes have been this year’s theme so it is no surprise that 13/3(2) is the correct play and 18/13(2), 18/8 is an error.
