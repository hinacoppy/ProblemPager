This looks frightening for White but actually, it is a very easy take.

He is 27 pips in the race and any hit by White could see him win a gammon or redouble Red out.

There is meaningful duplication of Red’s 1’s and 3’s and if Red hits in the outfield White could create an anchor on Red’s 3-pt or 5-pt or win from there.

It is the double that is in question and XG has this right on the borderline. Basically, either not redoubling or redoubling is correct. Of course, if you think your opponent might drop the redouble (and there are many who would) then redouble with a confident air and expect to receive two points!
