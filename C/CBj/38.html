Money Play. How should Red play 21?
pips Red=110 White=93
To win the game Red will need his 3-pt so lifting the blot is not an option despite the risk.

There are many different ways to play this 21 some of which involve de-duplicating Red's 5's (which he needs to escape) but many of those plays expose another blot and that could add to Red's gammons losses.

The best play is 10/7 which leaves no more blots and has one other benefit which may not be immediately obvious. When White enters and hits the blot on Red's 3-pt, Red has an effective prime against White's checker that could cause White's front position to collapse if he can't escape that checker. It is this that gives 10/7 the edge.
