Money Play. How should Red play 62?
pips Red=109 White=60
Red needs to maintain pressure on White’s mid-point and especially for White’s bad sixes.

Over the board Red played 13/5 but that is the wrong idea. Red should take tactical advantage of the blot on White’s 4-pt. The best play and the only one that isn’t an outright blunder is 20/14, 3/1 which puts the checkers precisely where Red wants them.

This is not a difficult problem once you see that you have to give up the 5-pt anchor but many would hold on to it precisely because we are taught not to give up anchors unnecessarily. Here the tactical demands of the position far outweigh standard practice.
