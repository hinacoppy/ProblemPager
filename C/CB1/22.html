This is not too difficult a problem if you have seen the idea before.

Red should not play safely with 13/5. Sixes don't grow on trees and his prime could collapse all too quickly if he doesn't roll another six very shortly.

Playing 22/16, 13/11 considerably helps his timing and even if White points on the Red checker left in White's home board the 4-pt will still be available for re-entry. Meanwhile Red has increased his chances of making a full prime.
