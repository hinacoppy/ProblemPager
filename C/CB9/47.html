The second blunder (this time nearly a triple) is to move 3/2 with the 1. Red still has a lot of freight to shift and as soon as White rolls a 3 he will become favourite. Red needs to make this a one-roll game if at all possible. Therefore the 1 must be played 8/7 (4/3 would duplicate Red’s 5’s).

If White rolls a 3 next turn he will probably win the game but if doesn’t he is staring down the barrel of defeat.
