I learned a good lesson here. I played 24/22(2) quite quickly and then 13/11(2) looked logical for the two 2’s. Given Red’s home board I was keen to advance to the edge of White’s prime.

Very wrong said XG. You should make your own four-point prime plus an additional home board point with 13/11, 8/4, 6/4, putting the checkers where they belong.

Because I thought 24/22(2) was mandatory I never considered XG’s play but once you see it becomes both logical and clear. The lesson is to consider all four quadrants of the board before selecting a move.

13/11, 8/4, 6/4 gives Red a better prime than White so that is the play he should make. 24/22(2), 13/11(2) is a bad blunder!
