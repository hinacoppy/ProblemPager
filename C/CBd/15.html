This is actually a borderline double and a massively trivial take.

Red wins just enough gammons to make the double correct and the other key and very important point is that White can hardly ever redouble very quickly because he still has to clear the mid-point and he has an open 4-pt.

That combination of factors give Red a skinny take but given that Red’s equity is only 0.187 after doubling I wouldn’t argue with a chouette partner who wanted to wait!
