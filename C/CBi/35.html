Money Play. How should Red play 51?
pips Red=141 White=122
I used to play these positions passively and hope my opponent would roll an awkward number and so I would play 11/5.

Over time I have come to understand that the strategy for playing against one rear checker, i.e. attack, is still correct in such positions. 7/6, 7/2* is the move here with 11/10, 7/2* a close second. 11/5 is a blunder above the 0.1 level. Notice that after the hit 21 and 24 aren't that great for White.

The hit is particularly correct because of White's exposed blot on his 1-pt. Give him his 1-pt by moving the spare checker from his 3-pt and then 11/5 and 7/6, 7/2* are just about equal. As ever, the detail matters.
