Without the Quiz Factor I think most people would play 11/3, 7/3(2) without much thought (as I did) but the alternative 11/7, 8/4, 6/2(2) is slightly better.

It duplicates fives and importantly doesn't leave another blot lying around in the variations where White rolls well. That extra blot adds to the gammon count and makes the difference between the two plays.

If you don't ‘see' a move you can't play it and that's what happened to me here.
