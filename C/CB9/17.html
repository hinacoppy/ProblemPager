2016 - Position 156

Money Play. How should Red play 44?
pips Red=146 White=122
Obviously two of the fours are played 18/10*. The question is which point to make in the home board.

Many would play 6/2(2) to utilise the two spare checkers on the 6-pt. However owning the 8-pt and 2-pt is not good backgammon because they can never be part of the same prime.

Correct is 7/3(2) which leaves Red with a much more flexible position. It is perhaps remarkable that 6/2(2) is a 1.5 blunder. Not making either point and playing 20/16(2) with the other two fours is double blunder.
