Continuing a theme this is another “pay me now, pay me later” problem. Again White is improving and the Red checkers on White’s bar-point are going to be difficult to clear.

This one is much closer but 18/15, 18/14 is the best play. 8/4, 6/3 just postpones the problem of the rear checkers and Red will soon need a double.

8/4, 6/3 is not even an error but it is not the best move. It is important to understand when to take risks like this. This one is a matter of fine judgement.

Put the spare White checker on Red’s 5-pt onto White’s 5-pt and 8/4, 6/3 is very clearly the best play and 18/15, 18/14 would be a bad blunder as White’s two outermost points are now stripped.
