Money Play. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=163 White=154
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I think a lot of people would play either 6/1 (which was played over the board) or 8/4, 6/5.

However, both of these moves are blunders. The race is close and Red’s three rearmost points are all stripped – often a danger sign. Couple this with the fact that White has only nine checkers in the attack zone and the 23/18 becomes clear.

It gives Red a working spare and he only needs one checker in White’s board to exert pressure on White’s 8-pt and 9-pt. As ever it’s simple once you see it!
