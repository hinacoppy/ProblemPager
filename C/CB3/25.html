2017 - Position 137

Money Play. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=144 White=133
The pure play is 21/11 but is too risky?

There is duplication of threes and Red’s four-point board is a good deterrent but four blots is a lot. Equally well Red has given away the cube and so must win with the checkers.

The alternative play is 7/3, 7/1 starting too more points and hoping for a two or a three next turn.

The two approaches are very different and it is hard, especially over the board, to evaluate the two game plans. Luckily on this occasion you can’t make a mistake! The two plays have nigh on identical equities so take your choice!
