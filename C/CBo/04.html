pips Red=143 White=74
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
If the cube were in the middle Red would make this a one roll game by playing 10/4* and then either 16/15 or 13/12.

With the cube already turned and gammons in play it is much more difficult because Red can play this as an attack or as prime versus prime with 16/10, 13/12. That play turns out to be a tiny error.

The best play is 10/4*/3 followed by 13/12, 10/4* which is only 0.006 behind. This shows that sometimes more than one play can be viable. Leaving the point slotted wins more games as you would expect but also loses more gammons. The two things balance out leaving you with a choice where either 10/4*/3 or 13/12, 10/4* can be deemed to be correct.
