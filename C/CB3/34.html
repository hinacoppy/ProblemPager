Money Game. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=143 White=120
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Not the roll Red was hoping for!

There is no better 5 than covering with 8/3. It is then a choice between 21/17, 13/9 or 8/4.

21/17 is the wrong idea because Red would like to make an anchor in White's home board and so he should leave both blots where they are. 8/4 allows a number of calamitous double hits and so by elimination we are left with 13/9 which creates another blot but at least duplicates White's 1's and 2's. Barring 11, 22 and 44 Red will only have one blot on the next time and a reasonable chance of survival.

The key thing here is to determine Red's plan before making your move.
