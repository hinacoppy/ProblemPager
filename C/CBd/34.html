Money Play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=108 White=148
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
6/3* is clear. Equally clear is that 3/2 is not the correct 1. Red has work to do and needs to close out White.

So after 6/3* we have the choice of 24/23, 9/8 and 4/3. The plays are very close and the difference lies in avoiding the anti-joker, 33, after either of the first two plays. This gives the nod, by a short head, to 4/3.

Needless to say I played 24/23 and my opponent rolled 33! In consequence the position ended up on my website!
