pips Red=133 White=88

Red cannot afford to weaken his board with something like 6/4 after entering bar/21 with the 4. This would give White carte blanche to attack. Red must keep his four-point home board and so the obvious 9/7, duplicating White's hitting ones, is correct, despite the slight increase in lost gammons that comes from this play.
