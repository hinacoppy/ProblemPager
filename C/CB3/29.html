b) Match Play. Red leads 1-0 to 7. Should Red redouble? If redoubled should White take?

Unlike the last position I posted this time the dice were with me. I redoubled at 0-1, my opponent took and my next two rolls were 54 and 16. I won a gammon easily and with it the match. But what about the cube decisions?

It turns out that this is a redouble (just) and a trivial take. Leading 1-0 it would be nearly an octuple blunder to redouble! The overage counts against Red and he should just play on, keep the cube and hope to win a gammon.

Small differences in the score in short matches lead to huge changes in the doubling decisions and this position is a classic demonstration of that fact.

By the way I seem to remember that in one of Kleinman's or Robertie's book there is the analysis to prove that Red's checker on the 23pt will take, on average, 5.5 rolls to escape.
