The position is highly volatile and by next turn White might be passing so Red should redouble now, although it is close.

White has plenty of ways to win this. He has a massive racing lead and a five-point home board which is effectively a five-point prime. Red has work to do but there are enough market losing sequences to make this a redouble.

If White’s two checkers on his 6-pt were redistributed to his 5-pt and 4-pt this wouldn’t be a redouble as White no longer has awkward numbers like 54.

In practical terms this is a great redouble – not everybody would take this despite the fact that it is a double blunder to pass the redouble.
