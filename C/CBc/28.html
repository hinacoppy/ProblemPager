Match Play. 0-0 to 5. For all scores in a 5-pt match other than Post-Crawford scores what is the cor
pips Red=12 White=12
Obviously for money this is a standard reference position where the the solution is double/redouble and take.

In a 5-pt match every point is precious, so much so that the correct action varies considerably according to the score. The correct actions for each non-Crawford score are as follows:

0-0    D/T
1-0    D/P
2-0    D/P
3-0    D/T

1-1    D/P
2-1    D/P
3-1    ND/T
3-2    D/P

0-1    D/T
0-2    D/T
0-3    ND/T

1-2    D/T
1-3    D/T
2-3    D/P

my | opponent score
score | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
------+------+------+------+------+
0 | D/T | D/T | D/T | ND/T |
------+------+------+------+------+
1 | D/P | D/P | D/T | D/T |
------+------+------+------+------+
2 | D/P | D/P | | D/P |
------+------+------+------+------+
3 | D/T | ND/T | D/P | |
------+------+------+------+------+

As you can see when a win can take one player to Crawford or the match you must take great care to think things through to come to the right decision.

As an example, leading 3-2 you may think you should take a double as the trailer because winning the game gets you to the match but you can never win with a redouble and letting your opponent get to 4-3 ahead (Crawford) is very bad for you.

This whole topic will be the subject of a longer article that I will write for the UKBGF a little later in the New Year.
