Money Play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=148 White=149
I got this one wrong. I played 23/22, 6/3* trying to escape and make sure that White didn’t get a 3-4 back game.

However, White doesn’t have the timing for the back game so you don’t really have to worry about it. The Red checker on the 23-pt should be viewed as an asset rather than liability and it can be used to pick up additional checkers if White’s four-point prime cracks.

The best play turns out to be the simple 16/12. Red should not strew blots around in his outer board to try to make new points. That strategy is too risky when White still has more home board points than Red.

13/12, 6/3* is the second-best play but still an error because it follows the wrong game plan. This was a difficult problem. Well done if you got it correct for the right reasons.
