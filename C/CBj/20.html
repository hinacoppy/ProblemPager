Match Play. Red leads 6-4 to 7 post-Crawford. How should Red play 51?
pips Red=112 White=115
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Over the board Red played 8/2 minimising shots but that was the wrong idea. He has three outside points to clear and so he should choose to attempt to clear the most difficult one now while White has a home board weakness.

To that end 13/12, 13/8 is the indicated play and this is much stronger than trying to clear either of the other points.

But should Red leave any shots? After all White has a four-point home board and a gammon loss would be ruinous. In the rollouts 6/1, 4/3 is only 0.011 weaker than 13/12, 13/8. That is certainly an interesting result and emphasises just how much consideration should be given when leaving voluntary shots against a four-point board.
