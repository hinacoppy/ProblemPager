Money Game. How should Red play 52?

Julian Fetterlein showed me this one at the UK Open.

Assuming no doubles by either side after the play Red can't afford to miss a crossover or he will be gammoned. The danger is that he may have to move a checker from his 7-pt deep into his board to make a crossover and will then come up short later on as he tries to get his straggler home.

It is far better to waste one pip now with bar/20, 7/5 than more pips later. What is perhaps surprising is that bar/20/18 is worse than a double blunder!!

Problems like this may not be exciting but they do help to teach fundamental technique. As a quiz problem virtually nobody will get this wrong. Over the board many will â€˜routinely' play bar/20/18 and never know they have blundered!
