The most likely scenario here is that Red will enter and bring down his checker from the mid-point. If White then fans he will be in severe gammon danger.

So much so that he must pass Red’s double – it is a double blunder to take. Red wins 65% of the time and of those wins 70% are gammons. That is far too high a price to pay.

Even if White does anchor on Red’s 5-pt he still trails in the race and has an inferior home board. Just too much has to go well for him to win the game.

If you didn’t double this position then you need to learn to look ahead a little better and think about the likelihood of probable scenarios. Of course Red will lose games from here but as the upside is a gammon he should be quick to double – I know a lot of people who would take as White!
