Money Play. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=115 White=129
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red can play relatively safely here with 9/5, 9/3 but White owns the cube so Red must win with the checkers alone. That implies a more aggressive move. White also has no anchor and that again cries out for a hitting play to take advantage of that fact and hopefully pick up some gammons.

After hitting with 8/2* Red should not play 8/4 with the 4 but should optimise his covering chances for next turn by playing 9/5.

9/5, 8/2* and 9/5, 9/3 win very nearly the same percentage of games but 9/5, 8/2* wins nearly another 11% gammons - a huge difference. Any play other than 9/5, 8/2* is at least a blunder.
