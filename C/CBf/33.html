Money Play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=118 White=138
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This position demonstrates a couple of concepts.

Red must hit with the 3, 7/4*. Not hitting gives White too many options.

Picking and passing, 7/4*/3 is very weak. Red will need his 4-pt to blitz White so he must leave it slotted. Many would then play 11/10 with the 1 to give another cover number next turn.

However, Red’s two rear checkers are behind a broken prime and now is the time to prepare an escape route or perhaps advance the anchor to White’s 4-pt where 6’s are not blocked.
