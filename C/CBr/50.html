Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=166 White=111
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I got this one well wrong over the board. I played 18/15, 18/13 which I think many would choose with the objective of maintaining the1-4 back game.

The problem is that Red needs to be about 90 pips behind in the race to play a 1-4 back game and at the moment he is well shy of that. With such dubious timing he needs to look for a different plan.

Clearly the ace-point should not be given up as that is the last bastion of Red’s defence. Equally well the checkers on the bar-point are holding the checkers on White’s mid-point and 11-pt.

This leads us to the unusual play of giving up Red’s 4-pt. The correct way to do this is to play 21/13, leaving the option of remaking the 4-pt later in some variations. 21/16, 21/18 is a blunder as in fact is every play other than 21/13.
