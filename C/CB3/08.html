Money Play. Should Red redouble? If redoubled should White take?
pips Red=140 White=158
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This was the final game of the night in Monday's Battersea chouette. The Box and his partner were White and actually held three 8 cubes and one 4 cube.

They redoubled all the cubes and the all the team, including World Number 2 Giant, Michi, took. Red rolled 44(!) played 9/5, 8/4(2), 7/3 and then white rolled 26: bar/23, 24/18.

Red then did escape a checker with a 6 but after an innocuous 32 from White, Red rolled 55 and it was all over. Red had to drop the redoubles to 32 and 16.

Given the initial position has a high percentage for both sides there was the potential for a huge swing on this game, as we have just seen.

The double by Red is clear because so many of White's entering numbers crunch his board. So what about the take? At the time we weren't sure but a long rollout establishes that this is a take by a small margin (0.039) and so dropping is a small error.

One disadvantage for the box was having Michi on the team. Once he took everyone else on the team was also likely to take. Practical chouette management!
