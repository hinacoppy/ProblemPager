Money Play. How should Red play 44?
pips Red=155 White=148
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Position submitted by Alastair Woods

What fun! There are just so many ways to play this 44. The tidy play is 13/5, 11/7(2)* but that leaves the back checkers stranded and a blot on the mid-point. Can Red do better?

The answer is yes. The second best play is 24/20(2), 6/2(2)*. However, as is often the case in backgammon, hitting two blots is best and 24/20, 11/7*, 6/2(2)* is best. If White doesn't hit the blot on the 5-pt the game is vey likely to be over very quickly.

It is the potential for doubling (and winning a gammon if White takes) that makes this the correct play.
