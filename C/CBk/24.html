Position initially posted by Phil Simborg.

The key in this position is tempo. Red cannot allow White his whole roll for attacking purposes. 13/7, 8/3, 6/5 and 8/7, 6/1 should all be considered but rejected.

The correct play, and the only one that isn’t at least a blunder is 8/2*. This takes away half (or all) of White’s next roll. If White rolls a 2 (except 11 and 21) then he still stuck behind Red’s prime and Red might benefit from anchoring in White’s home board. 8/2* also usefully duplicates White’s 3’s.

I don’t think this is an easy play to see but once again the old adage of “when in doubt, hit”, pays dividends.
