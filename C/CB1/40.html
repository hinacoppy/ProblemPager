(CB1-40)   2014 - Position 40
Double Match Point. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=80 White=109
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
For money the hit with 10/4, 2/1* is clear because it wins so many more gammons.

At DMP things are not so obvious. All Red needs is to win the game. If he plays 10/3 then all sixes other than 66 will leave a shot next turn, but that may never happen whereas 10/4, 2/1* gives White a shot immediately. More likely is that Red will leave a couple of indirect shots next turn.

With White having a four-point home board the hit turns out to be slightly too risky and 10/3 is the correct play.
