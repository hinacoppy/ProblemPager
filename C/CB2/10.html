Match Play. Red leads 4-1 to 5, Crawford Game. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=92 White=105
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is an object lesson in staying awake!

I had this position this week in a London League and for some reason made the money play 16/12*, 16/10. For money the hit is clear because it wins many more gammons (assuming the cub has been turned) but at Crawford Red should let White destroy his home board to maximise his game-winning rather than his gammon-winning chances.

As a result White got far more play than he should have and I was lucky to win the game. Lesson learnt!
