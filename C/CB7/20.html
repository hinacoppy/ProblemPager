One day someone will produce a formula for positions like this. The key factors are the number of points in Whiteâ€™s home board and the number of pips Red needs to get his checkers home.

The danger of course is that White will anchor or have time to throw the dreaded double fives. In this position Red only need 28 pips to get home but equally well White only has a two-point home board.

8/5*/4 is reasonable but best by a small margin is 10/9, 8/5 which provides maximum coverage for the blot if not hit and many returns if it is (except of course with 55!).

Give White his 2-pt and then 8/5*/4 wins by a whisker so this is a good benchmark position. File it for future use.
