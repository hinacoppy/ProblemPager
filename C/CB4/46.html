I had this position as Red against XG and quickly played 8/6, 8/4. In post-match analysis XG recommended 8/2, a move I hadn't considered. My move rated as an error which looks harsh!

Its advantage is that when the blot is hit it slows Red down and gives White the opportunity to wreck his board, thus reducing Red's risk of losing after a hit in the bear-off. In equity terms it is only fractionally better than 11/7, 8/6 and 11/7, 9/7 but the main point is to remind us all not to overlook a possible play.
