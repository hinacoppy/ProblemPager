One of my triple blunders! I (too) quickly played 9/5, 8/6 preparing to attack White’s ace-point blot next turn.

However, the priority is create outer board coverage in case White escapes with a 6. The only play that is not a blunder, and most of the other moves are double blunders, is 20/16, 20/18 which, as well as giving excellent outer board coverage, also provides mild duplication of White’s ones.

It’s easy once you see it but if you don’t see it in the first place (and I didn’t) then that is a problem!
