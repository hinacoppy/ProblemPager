What an awkward roll! The readers chose 17/11, 8/4.

However, minimising shots is not the point here. In such positions it is important to keep as many checkers in play as possible and that checker on the 8-pt doesn’t yet belong on the 4-pt.

When ahead in the race, race and so the logical play is 22/16, 17/13. There is no duplication but Red doesn’t really fear being hit. 17/11, 8/4 is the second best play but it an error. Any other play is a blunder. The key thing here is to understand the necessary strategy and that is to mobilise those rear checkers.
