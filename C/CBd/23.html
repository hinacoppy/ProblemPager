Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=122 White=113
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The position is certainly volatile and Red should clearly be doubling.

Equally well White has a perfectly viable take. He has a four-point prime (“Four is more than you think’ – Kent Goulding) and those spare checkers on his 3-pt can still find a useful home.

Occasionally White will get blitzed – hence he loses a gammon 30% of the time - but when he anchors or hits back he is well in the game and will win about 37% of the time. Red also has some interesting checkers plays coming up. How would you play 43, 41 or 33? Get those wrong and you could lose a lot of equity.

Always remember to consider your opponent’s skill level when taking/dropping cubes.
