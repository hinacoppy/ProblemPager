c) Money Play. After double/take how should Red play 43?
d) Money play. After no double how should Red play 43?


a) For money this is a very clear double and a very clear take. The position is very volatile and gammonish but White's structure gives him a take.
b) The fact that a gammon wins Red the match exactly should set White's alarm bells ringing. This fact pushes the decision to double/pass although it is close.
c) Once the cube has been turned Red must win with checkers alone so 13/9, 6/3* is correct.
d) If Red has forgotten to double then he should play 8/4*/1 as gammons are not activated and 13/9, 6/3* is an overplay in this variation.
