I am sure that over the board many would play 10/4, 8/7* but, as noted in the comments by Tim Chow, Red runs the danger of crashing his home board if he doesn't quickly roll a 5 or a 6.

Red does better to adopt a two-way game plan. 23/17 maintains his timing and 8/7* attempts to win by going forwards. White's next roll will determine which game plan is put into action.

10/4, 8/7* is an error but not a blunder. 23/17, 5/4 is too passive and basically commits to a back game and XG rates this as a double blunder.
