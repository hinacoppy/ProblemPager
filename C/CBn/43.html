Match Play. 0-0 to 7. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=94 White=147
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
You will often hear people say that all prime versus prime positions are takes but of course that is not quite the case!

This is very clearly a redouble – any six loses Red’s market by a country mile. For money White would have to drop this redouble. There are just too many gammons. Also note that if White enters his checker quickly all twos except 21 and 26 force him to break his prime.

You might think at 0-0 to 7 the match score is not a consideration but it has a significant influence. A doubled gammon gives either player one more point than he needs. This overage turns a pass into a comfortable take for White and it would actually be a blunder to pass (which is what my opponent did).

The lesson here is that once unnecessary points are part of the equation you need to take very careful account of the match score.
