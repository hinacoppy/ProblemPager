Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=45 White=48
I had this position in an online match and played 43, 4/1. This turned out to be the fourth best play.

While the play is the safest it also means that if Red rolls a 5 or a 6 next turn he will have to open another point. After the best play 6/5, 6/3 that is not necessarily the case and that is enough to make 6/5, 6/3 the winner.

However 3/off, 1/off is only 0.012 behind as the shot left is virtually compensated for by the two extra checkers off. An interesting piece of education!
