This comes from Hickey and Storer’s “What’s Your Game Plan?” which I recommend that everybody should read as it will help to improve your understanding of middle game strategies and priorities.

I think most people’s instinctive reaction here would to be to play bar/9 leaving 13 shots. However, Red will still trail in the race after the play and it gives White a number of different game plans.

Much better is to take the solid of the asset of the 9-pt with bar/21, 13/9(3). That gives Red a broken prime and the rear blot may well hamper White’s development. White will naturally attack the Red blot and Red may well lose but he is not the favourite whatever he plays. The key is to make the play that wins most often and that play is bar/ 21, 13/9(3).
