For money 20/14 is a perfectly reasonable play and it ties with 13/11, 13/9 and 7/3, 6/4.

But, as ever, the match score dominates the thinking and 20/14 is a bad error at this score. It lead to some gammon losses and therefore to the loss of the match. That is too high a price to pay and so 13/11, 13/9 is the best play but 7/3, 6/4 and even 13/7 are not far behind. The key is to improve the forward position before running off the anchor.
