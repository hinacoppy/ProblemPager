Match Play. Red leads 1-0 to 5. How should Red play 21?
pips Red=70 White=218
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Playing on automatic you would just clear the bar-point with 7/6, 7/5 but a little study will show you that 6/5, 6/4 is the correct move. It clears the awkward 6-pt but more importantly it forces White to play his fives from Red’s ace-point and give up that anchor when he doesn’t want to.

This play increase Red’s gammon percentage. Even with a won position you must play accurately. You may be surprised to learn that White actually wins from here nearly 12% of the time. He only has two dead checkers so he can still build a full home board.
