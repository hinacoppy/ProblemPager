Only two choices here: bar/20/14 or bar/20, 13/7.

Red needs to keep control of the outer boards so that White cannot easily escape his rear checker. To do that he must play bar/20/14 despite the double shot.

Bar/20, 13/7 gives up all outfield control plus letting White hit with any 1 or 2 and double ones and double twos are ruinous.

Bar/20, 13/7 is nearly a double blunder.
