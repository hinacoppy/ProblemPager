Money Play. How should Red play 21?
pips Red=158 White=136
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
‘Put them where you want them’ to quote Kent Goulding.

This roll allows Red to optimise the use of his spare checkers on his 6-pt and 5-pt. Meanwhile, White is by no means guaranteed to make his own 4-pt next time so Red might have the opportunity to hit next time. Otherwise he can play this as prime versus prime where he has edge in timing.

The decision is relatively close but it is definitely an error to hit on the 4-pt – note that White has a superior home board so may well come out ahead in a hitting context.

This is an instructive position so heed its lesson well!
