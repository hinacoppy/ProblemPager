Money Game. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=128 White=138
Phil Simborg, backgammon teacher and guru, very kindly regularly sends out interesting positions from his chouette and he was happy for me to re-use this one.

Should Red break the â€˜Dimitri Rule' (never double without a new home board point). He leads in the race, has cleared his rear checkers and has a broken five-point prime. Coupled with White's messy home board structure surely all that adds up to a double?

Indeed it does but what I didn't appreciate when I first looked at this position was just how weak White's position is. If he had an anchor on Red's 3-pt then the position is only just a double but the problem for White is that he can be blitzed as well as all his other problems. That eventuality turns the position into a huge pass - it's a double blunder to take.

Phil normally only posts positions where someone errs so I assume one or more optimists took this one!
