Red's position is not as good as it looks. If the blot on the 24-pt were on the 23-pt then this would be redouble/big pass but that one pip makes all the difference. On average it will take Red 5.5 rolls to escape that blot from behind the prime.

Meanwhile things can go badly wrong for him on the rest of the board and he needs some improvement before he can redouble. For money a redouble here would be a quadruple for blunder. The match score waters that down to merely a simple blunder!

Of course if you think your opponent might drop that is another thing altogether!
