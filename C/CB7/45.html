Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=179 White=64
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
When I had this position in a Gridgammon match I’ll be the first to admit I had no idea of the correct play and given the speed of online play I didn’t have enough time to work it out from first principles. In my extensive library of positions I don’t have one like this.

With the cube in play I would have just built my board with 12/6, 8/5 as that play undoubtedly loses fewer gammons but does one of the hitting plays win more games at DMP? I just didn’t know so I applied the principle of when in doubt, hit and went gung-ho with the double hit 22/17*, 21/18*.

This turns out to be a double blunder – it loses more games and more gammons! The single hit 22/17*, 8/5 is much better but virtually any non-hitting play is better and 12/6, 8/5 best of all. It turns out that waiting is the correct policy here.
In the actual game I was very lucky and in about 8 moves, including a great 66, I had three of my opponents checkers closed out on the bar. Such is backgammon!
