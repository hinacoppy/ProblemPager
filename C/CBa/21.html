(CBa-21)   2015 - Position 170
Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 63?
pips Red=151 White=133
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Hitting with 23/14* is far too loose whether in money or match play.

For money 10/4, 7/4 edges out the other plays because it increases the gammon percentage.

However, this is DMP and gammons don’t count. It turns out that the two top plays both make Red’s bar-point – not something I got right over the board. 13/10, 13/7 is best followed by 18/15, 13/7. The idea is to prime White’s rear checkers. 13/7, 10/7 is a tad conservative as the checkers all need to work at DMP and it is not clear a third checker on the bar-point is correct.

The equity differences aren’t huge and 10/4, 7/4 is not even an error but the important thing from this position is to learn how to adjust your thinking for DMP – not something we necessarily do as well as we should.
