The non-thinking play is 15/13, 6/5 but that does nothing to improve Red’s position.

He trails in the race and holds White’s 5-pt so nothing really bad can happen to him. He should be seeking to strengthen his forward position and the best way to do that is to make his 10-pt. Ergo the obvious (and correct) play is 13/10.

15/14, 13/11 and 13/11, 6/5 are also reasonable plays but the unthinking 15/13, 6/5 is very nearly a blunder.
