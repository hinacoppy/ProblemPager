If you play this as prime vs prime you get the right answer.

Knock White away from the edge of the prime with 21/3*. Maintain the four-point prime so the logical fourth 6 is 22/16. Playing the fourth six with 7/1 is a big blunder. To quote Kent Goulding “four is more than you think”.

Running with all four checkers is the wrong strategy. Red is still 15 pips behind after the roll so follow the old adage: “when behind in the race, don't race”.
