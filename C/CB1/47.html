Match Play. Red leads 2-0 to 5. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=105 White=177
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
For money this would obviously be a very clear double and a huge pass but the match score changes everything.

Losing a gammon doesn't really hurt White that much as he will only give up about 15% match winning chances. However, he will be redoubling very quickly if he begins to turn the game around.

Admittedly White's position is very poor but as he has no checkers out of play he can rebuild it.

Technically this is just a double but it is also a trivial take. This is the sort of position where it pays to know your opponent. I did and I knew he would take later so I held the cube. As it happened I played on and won a gammon very easily.
