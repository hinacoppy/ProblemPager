Match Play. Red leads 6-2 to 9. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=137 White=147
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
After your play what is the correct cube action for both players.


Red has doubled so needs to win with the checkers alone. Passive plays like 22/11 aren’t good enough. He needs to hit with the 5, 13/8*. Then the logical 6 is to escape a checker with 22/16, despite leaving three blots. If not hit back Red’s position will have improved considerably.

At this match those three blots give White enough volatility to redouble. Any hit from the bar and he could win an eight point gammon and the match. White should recognise that being behind 2-8 to 9 leaves him very low winning chances so he should take the risk of redoubling now. In fact not redoubling is a blunder.
