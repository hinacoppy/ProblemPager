Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=72 White=62
Red needs to combine limiting White’s hitting numbers with maintaining his racing chances.

That is best done with 18/16, 6/2. Staying on White’s bar-point is an error. After 18/16, 6/2 eight of White’s 6’s (which are good for the race) are poor, while if he hits Red retains good hitting chances of his own.

Move the two checkers from Red’s ace-point to his bar-point and 6/4, 6/2 becomes the right play as Red’s racing chances have diminished considerably.
