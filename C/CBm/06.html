Match Play. 2-2 to 5. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=143 White=18
Well the 3 is clear - 24/21*!

After that Red can reasonably play 21/17, 24/20, 8/4 or 7/3 with the 4.

Keeping the pure five-point prime is correct as this gives White one great number (63) as opposed to two (61 and 52) so we can eliminate 7/3. It will also be easier for Red to complete the full prime if he doesn't play 7/3.

Should Red give up the 24-pt anchor so that White's 32 is horrendous? Over the board I thought so and played 24/20 but ceding the point is premature and it gives White a great double 1. It is better to keep the point and let White roll an ace some time later and have to play 3/2. 21/17 is better than 8/4 (just) but it is very close and either play is OK.

I was seduced by the immediate opportunity of a 32 by White but Red can play the longer game here with 24/21*/17. 24/21*, 24/20 is a bad blunder.
