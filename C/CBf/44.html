Money Play. How should Red play 62?
pips Red=161 White=136
This position is interesting because the best play could possibly be viewed as anti-thematic.

It is unusual to race when behind but here 24/16 is the correct. Red sacrifices some long-term equity by moving off the White ace-point but he gains in two ways: he has greater coverage of the outer boards and he can profit from an exchange of hits in White’s outer board.

13/5, taking the last spare checker off the mid-point and potentially letting White’s rear checker escape with a tempo hit, is the wrong idea.
