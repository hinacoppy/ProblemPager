Match Play. Red trails 2-3 to 7. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=13 White=12
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)

Most people don’t do this over the board either because they can’t or because they haven’t got the time. I am able to do these calculations but only do so in major tournaments as they take time and certainly on Gridgammon people would become impatient. I use the Janowski formula to do my match equity calculations.

Without doing the calculations I would reason as follows:

This is somewhere around a three-roll position but with lots of potential misses for both sides so it could actually be a 1,2, 3 or 4-roll position.

As Red if I redouble and win I will be 6-3 (Crawford) ahead and my opponent is on an odd number of points ahead which is very good for me.

If I redouble and lose then I lose the match which is obviously bad for me buy hey you can't win them all.

On roll and 1 pip behind I am favourite probably somewhere between 60 and 70%.

The position is highly volatile so ‘taking a roll’ looks wrong – I should nearly certainly redouble now.

Finally apply Woolsey’s Law and give the problem to White – Redouble!

In fact Red is about 65% in this position. The take is trivial but the redouble is massively correct – it is more than a double blunder not to redouble.
