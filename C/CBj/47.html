After some difficult problems this one is somewhat easier. Red should play 6/2, 6/1, avoiding any complications when Red rolls a 1 after any of the other plays.

Go back to the 1970s and this would have been played 13/4 or 13/9, 13/ or even 8/4, 8/3. Nowadays we understand the value of not leaving blots when the victory is close.
