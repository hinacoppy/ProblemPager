Money Game. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=90 White=54
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
My partner in the box and I spent a couple of minutes arguing about this one. I wanted 6/2, 6/1 and he wanted 21/12.

The former works well when White rolls a 4, particularly 64, but then containment is not guaranteed. 21/12 is the pure play, virtually guaranteeing the win after a hit.

So who was right? Actually both of us. The pros and cons of the two moves trade off exactly and after over 5,000 rollouts the two plays were separated by 0.001 equity points.

I included the position merely to prove that sometimes, although rarely, there is more than correct answer to a backgammon problem.
