Money Play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=165 White=156
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The first two threes are obviously played bar/22(2).

The choice is then is 13/10(2), 8/5(2) or 6/3(2). It is always best to make the 5-pt if possible and this position is no exception. White’s blots on his bar-pt and particularly his ace-point mean that Red doesn’t need to have any fear of being hit.

If White’s bar-point were made then it would be very close between 8/5(2) and 6/3(2) (inner board points are normally better than outer board points) but, as it stands, any play other than bar/22(2), 8/5(2) is a blunder.
