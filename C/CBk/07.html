Money play. How should Red play 66? After your play are you taking White's double?
pips Red=173 White=119
Thanks to Simon Woodhead for the trial version of his new version of bglog which creates the diagrams on this site. Note the highlights on the red checkers and the turn indicator (bottom right). There are other new features which I haven't yet used.

There is only one play which lets Red take White's impending double and that is 22/16(2), 13/7(2).

This play keeps the pressure on White's blots, doesn't kill any checkers and keeps a measure of connectivity with the checker stranded on the 24-pt. The play loses a higher percentage of gammons than other plays but crucially the play also wins 33% of the time. All other plays are just too passive to let Red accept the cube.

It takes bravery to take positions such as this but that is what winning backgammon is all about.
