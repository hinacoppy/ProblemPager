Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=111 White=83
Red will need to take some risks to remake his 6-pt which is the crucial point on the board.

In live play 14/8 was played with the idea of creating a broken prime but this turns out not to be aggressive enough.

In a money game and holding a 2 cube Red should play17/13, 14/12, leaving only 3 shots and reducing the gammon risk. Here, however, gammons are irrelevant, and Red does best to spread his checkers while White is on the bar.

17/11 is fine but best by a tiny margin is 17/15, 13/9. The specifics of this position are, of course, important but much more important is to learn the technique of spreading the checkers in such situations. High risk garners high reward.
