Money Play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=63 White=81
Positions like this are commonplace.

It is not right to preserve 4’s and 5’s by playing 4/1, 3/2 which leaves home board blots. Red will be running from the 15-pt with most big numbers next turn.

Correct is to keep the four-point home board for the relatively unlikely event of a hit but ensuring that if there is a hitting sequence there are no home board blots left exposed.

The simple 6/2 is best. 4/1, 3/2 is a bad error but not quite a blunder.
