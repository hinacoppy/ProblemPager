When I was a young man I used to be hesitant about doubling positions like this (what if my opponent anchored?) and I would snap up the cube if doubled.

Age has given me wisdom (and a lot of practice and study) and I now know to double these positions in a flash and to drop them just as quickly. It's a triple blunder not to double and worse than a triple blunder to take. Even using something as simple as PRaT (Position, Race, Threat) gives you the right answer.
