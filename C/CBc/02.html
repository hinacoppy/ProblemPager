Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=94 White=85
So do you waste pips with 8/2, 6/1 or make a run for it with 20/9?

Both plays have advantages and disadvantages, the latter losing out to White’s small numbers while the former creates a poor bear-off position.

It turns out that these things balance out and there is only 0.006 equity between the two plays with 8/2, 6/1 winning by a whisker. So long as you didn’t go for the cute 20/14, 8/3 which achieves duplication for duplication’s sake then you did alright!
