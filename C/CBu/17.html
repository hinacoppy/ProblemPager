Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=157 White=175
This is a very unusual position and a detailed analysis would be very long.

Red will win a good percentage of gammons (actually 26%). White’s home board is a bit of a mess. However, he does hold that crucial 5-pt anchor in Red’s home board so cannot be blitzed and Red does only have a two-point home board.

The position is not very volatile, and I would hold on to the cube unless I thought my opponent would pass (not likely, that would be a quadruple blunder).

The rollout says you can double or not double and you will be making the right decision. As I said, I would play on against all but the most timid opponents – hopefully there will be better opportunities to double later and give my opponent a much more difficult decision.
