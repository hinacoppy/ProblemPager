Money Play. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=88 White=169
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Firstly, Red must recognise that he is not the favourite in this position although it’s close.

Over the board 10/8, 10/7 was played but move that doesn’t meet the demands of the position.

Red will need his 5-pt. 8/5, 7/5 is too big a play but clearing the 8-pt or 7-pt, while slotting the 5-pt is likely to be correct.

In fact, 8/6, 8/5 is the clear winner. It clears the more awkward point and keeps the spares on the 6-pt. The move provides very real duplication of 2’s and it is basically a pay-me-now play. Any other move is at least an error and 10/8, 10/7 is nearly a double blunder.
