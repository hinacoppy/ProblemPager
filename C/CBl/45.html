2014 - Position 85

Money Play. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=131 White=124
Level in the race after the play the first instinct for Red is to stay.

However, Red's forward checkers are all well placed and moving them doesn't really improve his position. Red also runs the danger of White taking command of the outer boards if he can run a checker from Red's 4-pt.

It is better for Red to take command of the outer board and hope he can withstand White's attack. Red must realise the game is very evenly balanced and that he should seek to turn it in his favour. Time is running out for his back checkers so now is the time to go. After that play Red will win a little over 50% of the time.

Interestingly and what you might find surprising is that any play that does not involve running a back checker is at least a double blunder.
