This problem may stump even very strong players, who will see that Black's strategic goal is to escape that back checker and avoid getting any other checkers sent back.
Even at the cost of a triple shot, 23/18 should present itself as a tempting candidate. (See for example the variant position below where 23/18 8/7 is the top play.)
However, certain peculiar features of this particular position collude to make 23/22 8/3* the top play by a surprisingly large margin.
Shift White's outfield blot forwards a pip or two, or give White cube ownership, or move one of the spares from Black's 8pt to his 7pt, and hitting becomes much less attractive.
