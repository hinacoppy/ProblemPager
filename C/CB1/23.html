The Othello Quiz is traditionally very difficult and last year's was no exception This was Question 1 and like Tim Chow in his comment to this position my instinctive reaction was to play 4/2*/1.

The problem with this idea is that Red doesn't have enough ammunition in the attack zone to carry out a blitz. Even if White fans with both checkers after 4/2*/1 he has a trivial take of Red's double.

White has a strong home board with reasonable development potential. As I have stressed before you should not engage in a war on two fronts so plays like 24/21 and 9/6 can be discounted. Red should take the anchor in White's home board with 24/23 and then the simple 13/11 is best with the 2.

Any play other than 24/23, 13/11 is a blunder. 24/23, 13/11 is the only move that has Red above 50% to win the game.
