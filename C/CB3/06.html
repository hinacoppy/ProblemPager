Match Play. Red leads 8-3 to 11. Estimate the equity loss if Red redoubles to 4 in this position.
pips Red=78 White=43
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I posted this as a bit of fun as it actually occurred in the quarter-final of the Pro Tournament at the London Open. It is the worst match play redouble I have seen in 35 years of playing the game.

The Italian Red was playing Michi (World number 2). After an innocuous first roll, a redouble to 8 and a fan by White, Red's next rolls were 41, 66, 55 and 55 winning very easily.

XG rates this as an equity error of -2, the equivalent of giving away an undoubled gammon. Given White only needs 7% to take the redouble it clear Red is miles away from a redouble.

I spoke to the player of Red after the match and he said he just wanted to finish the match against such a strong player! Not great thinking but you can understand where he is coming from.
