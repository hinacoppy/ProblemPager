Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 66?
pips Red=115 White=90
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
With no cube and no gammons Red must make the play the play that gives him the best chance of winning the game.

He can play for contact with 23/17(2), 8/2(2) or simply race with 23/11(2).

The problem with playing for contact is that he may have to break first and/or he may have to crash his home board which will diminish his racing chances.

The racing play 23/11(2) turns out to be best although he enters the race one pip behind. This play wins 40% of the time, the contact play only wins 38.6% of the time. QED, 23/11(2) is correct.
