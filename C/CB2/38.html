For money the extra gammon losses occurred by 13/9, 6/3 means that by a narrow margin 13/6 is the right play.

At DMP gammons are of no concern and therefore you want to make the play that wins the game most often. This is a classic prime vs prime and the checkers need to work to maximum efficiency as the cube is out of play.

That makes 13/9, 6/3 the correct play by a short head. 13/6 is not an error but 13/9, 13/10, leaving three blots is too wide open and is an error.
