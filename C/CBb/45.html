pips Red=141 White=145

How is Red going to win this?

He could mount a blitzing attack but his ammunition is not perfectly placed and White’s prime should give him pause for thought.

In fact, Red does best by trying to counter-prime White and force White’s prime to crack. The simple 8/5(2), 6/3(2) is by far the best play. Anything else is a blunder or worse. The half-way house play of 13/10, 11/5, 8/5, which was played over the board is a double blunder.

This is a position where taking the asset, rather than the threat of making an asset, is very important. Red wants his own prime as quickly as possible. You have herd it before but “Put them where where you want them” is still one of the most powerful adages in backgammon.
