This one caused a long, long debate at the Battersea chouette.

There are arguments for many plays including 22/16(2), 8/2(2) and 22/16, 4/1(2)*.

With the cube on White's side Red does best to follow Magriel's Maxim: “Put them where you want them”. This implies that 8/2(2) is the best move, as those checkers surely belong on the 2-pt, and indeed it is. 22/19(2), 22/16 is also good as that gives excellent outfield coverage. 22/16(2) would be a small error.

In the actual game the cube was on Red's side because none of the team elected to redouble – despite the that fact the position is only a whisker away from being too good to double! With the cube on Red's side the best play is 22/16(2) and the other plays become errors, thus showing that must adjust you play to the position of the cube. 22/16(2) gives great outfield coverage and ensures Red can redouble next turn (in most variations).
