This is all about command and control of the outer boards.

Bar/20 is forced. Take a slap on the wrist if you chose 6/2 for the 4. Backgammon is all about keeping checkers in play and Red certainly doesn’t want/need a third checker on his 2-pt.

The world’s #1 player, Mochy, chose 11/7 but that, perhaps surprisingly, is nearly a double blunder.

Bar/20/16 leaves a lot of shots but most hits also leave a lot of return hits. Importantly it fights for control of those outer boards. Red’s 22-pt anchor keeps him in the game for a long time so he does best by trying to put his checkers to work. As he is behind in the race he welcomes a sequence of hits.

Once you understand the logic bar/20/16 is clear but it is certainly not obvious at first sight.
