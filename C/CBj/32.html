Money Play. How should Red play 52?
pips Red=116 White=28
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The key point here is that Red should not come off White’s ace-point to create bad 3’s for White’s. Yes, he may get a shot but it may well be his last one. White already has bad 1’s so Red should stay put on the ace-point.

If White had three checkers on his 4-pt then Red should play 24/22, 14/9 to give White disastrous 43, 53 and 63 but even then it’s close.

In the given position the correct play is 14/9, 4/2, 12/5 or 14/7.
