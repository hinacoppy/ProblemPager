Another position whereat least two game plans are viable. Not of course the dreadful 24/20(2), racing when behind.

Red has the option of making two good points with 13/11(2), 6/4(2) or making his four point with 6/4(2) and then moving one or more back checkers. 24/22(2) is an error because White owns his 9-pt and it gives White space to play behind Red. Better is 24/20, challenging White to safety his blot immediately.

The two plays are virtually identical in equity with 13/11(2), 6/4(2) winning by a short head.
