pips Red=43 White=84

A massive pass for money can become a take in match play when the cube gets high and that is the case here. Alastair Woods has written a very good synopsis so I suggest you have a read of that.

It may surprise many that Red should actually redouble here and put the match on the line but it potentially removes White’s 9% winning chances from 0-6 behind and I’ve lost many a match from 6-0 up to 7!

Red is well into the doubling window but White has a comfortable take. Good match play requires a solid understanding of match equity tables and their application. How did you do? Would you have dropped as White? Would you have doubled as Red?
