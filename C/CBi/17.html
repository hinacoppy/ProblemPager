Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=66 White=169
Not all backgammon is complex play or doublings decisions. Plays like this come under the heading of technical accuracy.

If not hit Red is pretty much assured of a gammon so he should be looking for the safest long-term play. Once his 6-pt is cleared his chances of leaving a shot are very much reduced and as White has no home board he wants to leave it sooner rather than later.

All of this points to 10/5, 6/3 and that is the right play by a country mile. 10/5, 3/off is very nearly a blunder which might surprise some but once you think through the logic of the play, 10/5, 6/3 becomes very clear.
