pips Red=134 White=190
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is from my Consolation Final against Tariq Sadiqqi at Scope this weekend.

I looked at all the moves suggested by those who have already commented and the choice comes down to: 18/7, running while White's board is very weak; 18/13, 8/2* - escaping a checker and trying to deny White a second anchor; 13/8, 13/7 clearing the mid-point and subsequently looking to build the 4-pt; 8/2*, 7/2 â€“ the brute force play going for the gammon and trying to stop White building a second anchor.

In the end I went for 8/2*, 7/2 hoping to subsequently pick up the blot on the 9-pt as well. XG votes this as the right play but only by a little over 18/7. 18/13, 8/2* is an error and 13/7, 13/8 a blunder.

8/2*, 7/2 wins more gammons than any of the other plays and crucially gets Red to the Crawford Game with White on an odd number of points.

Some time later I had six of Tariq's men behind a five-point prime but then I lost the game from there! However, I did win the match 9-8.
