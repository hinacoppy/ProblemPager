pips Red=113 White=134
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one came up in a lesson and the result surprised me. Clearly it is a huge double for Red, but the duplication of fours gives White a comfortable take. I would have dropped.
White’s good home board and structure are sound and holding the 11-pt helps. White may anchor on the ace-point and win from there. With Red’s 20-pt racing lead it still looks like a pass to me, but XG assures me it is an easy take.
If you move the White checker from his 9-pt to his 10-pt it becomes a big pass. Duplication matters!
