(CB4-07)   2014 - Position 157
Money Game. How should Red play 52?
pips Red=125 White=105
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
A fascinating position that combines elements of the blitz with prime vs prime.

Should Red be going all out for the gammon by hitting 4/2* combined with either 13/8 (bringing a builder) or 23/18 (escaping the broken prime), or playing more quietly with 15/10, 13/11 or 15/10, 5/3?

Many would be fearful of hitting here because a return hit by White could spell a gammon loss for Red but equally well a fan by White will lead to a lot of gammon wins for Red.

A key point in the analysis is the fragility of White's prime which will crack on entering numbers such as 31, 41, 32 etc. Take a checker from White's 1-pt and put it on his 6-pt and any play other than 23/18, 4/2* is a big blunder as now White's broken prime is much more likely to survive.

In the actual position the waiting plays and the hitting plays have very similar equities. In a long rollout 23/18, 4/2* beats 15/10, 5/3 by 0.008 points of equity. The extra game and gammon wins for Red trade off against the extra gammon losses nearly exactly so the position becomes an interesting benchmark.
