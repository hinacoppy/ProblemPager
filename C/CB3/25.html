Match Play. Red leads 2-1 to 9. How should Red play 66?
pips Red=159 White=164
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Sometimes the Quiz Factor works in favour of the setter! Given this over the board nearly everyone would make both bar-points but given it as a problem there is a tendency to overanalyse and come up with justifications for other plays.

Here you should take what the dice give you and make the two bar-points with 24/18(2), 13/7(2). Even if trailing heavily in a match it would be the right play. 13/7(2), 8/2(2)* gives up 4.5% game winning chances. Any play other is a blunder and by the time you reach 8/2(2)*, 13/1* you are in double blunder territory.
