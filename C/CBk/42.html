Match Play. Red leads 3-1 to 5. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=154 White=147
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
24/18, 10/9 looks OK but it is not in reality. Red is outboarded and that play beautifully diversifies White’s hitting numbers.

13/7, 8/7 is the correct play with the plan of priming White’s rear checkers. That play is correct both for money and at this match score. The difference is that at this match score 13/7, 8/7 is the only play that lets Red take White’s double. For money White doesn’t have a double after either play. Such is the power of the cube when training 4-away vs 2-away.
