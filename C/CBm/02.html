pips Red=162 White=149
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one is a close call.

21/11, 6/1(2)* looks very logical, escaping a checker, putting White on the bar and leaving no meaningful return shots.

Is it worth the extra return shots to build a second home point board with 8/3(2), 6/1(2)*? Even if White fans Red does not have a double.

XG rollouts have 8/3(2), 6/1(2)* better by a tiny margin because of the additional gammons won but I am not convinced that Red can double efficiently enough (with the Jacoby Rule in effect) to get the benefit of those gammons. Over the board I played 21/11, 6/1(2)* and would do so again.

Note however that in match play with no Jacoby Rule in effect 8/3(2), 6/1(2)* is right by quite a long way because of the extra gammons won.
