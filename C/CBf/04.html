Match Play. Red trails white 6-8 to 11. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=136 White=123
With prime versus prime positions the normal approach is to come to the edge of your opponentâ€™s prime. That would indicate the 4 should be played bar/21 and then the natural 2 is 9/7.

However, prior to the roll both players have a four-point prime and Red could play bar/23, 13/9 creating a five-point prime with the sixth (the 10-pt) slotted. Meanwhile his rear checker would be more or less out of the line of fire.

These advantages far outweigh coming to the edge of the prime. So much so that bar/21, 9/7 is worse than a triple blunder.

In backgammon general rules and principles will get you a long way but each position has specific features that can easily outweigh the general principles. So, take care!
