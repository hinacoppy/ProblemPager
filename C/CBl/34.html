2014 - Position 96

Match Play. Red leads 4-2 to 7. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=138 White=118
At first glance you might drop this as White, but that 20-pip racing lead is big and Red has few market losers. White will split his back checkers with any 1 or 2 and his hitting chances combined with his racing chances give him a trivial take.

It is more Red's double that is in question because the position is relatively non-volatile and if White redoubles to 4 and wins he will be at Crawford.

XG has this as a double but only just and nobody could be criticised for holding on to the cube. Of course if you think your opponent might drop then that is another matter altogether.
