pips Red=130 White=130
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
There are two game plans here: a) run both back checkers while White has two home board blots by playing 21/16, 21/15 or b) hold everything and clear the mid-point with 13/8, 13/7.

I had this position in a match situation where running both checkers was clearly correct because of the increase in gammons.

In a money game both plans are viable and running the two rear checkers is better by a mere 0.017 equity points over the safe play. This just goes to show that sometimes two very different plans can, for all intents and purposes, be correct.
