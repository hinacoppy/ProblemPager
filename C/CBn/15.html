This is not obvious. The 1 should clearly be played 6/5. The problem is the 2.

6/4 is the poorest choice as it does nothing to improve Red’s position. The real choices are 5/3 or 22/20. The former boldly slots the point Red wants to make next while 22/20 prepares to escape and (potentially) avoids problems on future fours.

Over the board I played 6/3 but really wasn’t sure. It turns out the two plays have equal equities, demonstrating once again that there can be more than one viable plan in a position. Take full credit for either play.
