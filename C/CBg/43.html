Money Play. How should Red play 22?
pips Red=115 White=147
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red wants to dismantle his outside points but also wants to force White off his ace-point.

With this roll he can do either by playing 10/8, 10/4 or 10/8(2), 3/1(2)*.

The latter does both things but leaves an ungainly position while the former leaves an excellent structure.

It should perhaps come as no surprise that the two moves are tied in equity terms. Anything else is at least an error.
