A simple pay me now, pay me later problem.

18/10 is very clear as after 8/6, 8/2 Red’s checkers are disconnected and he will be struggling next turn. Meanwhile the blot on White’s ace-point is important. If White’s spare checker on his 2-pt was on his ace-point giving him a five-point home board then 8/6, 8/2 would be right by a long way.

As usual in backgammon, every pip matters. 18/10 it is.
