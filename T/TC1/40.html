Once again, the match score makes a difference.
It might seem natural to play either 10/7, preventing White from escaping her straggler with a 6, or 16/13 7/5, which minimizes shots.
Both of these would be reasonable plays at double match point (see Variant 1 below). At this score, gammons are valuable, so Black needs to consider the gammonish plays 7/2 (making a five-point board) and 4/2 4/1*. The latter sacrifices too many winning chances, leaving 7/2 as the rollout winner.
Note also that one important disadvantage of 16/13 7/5 is that it stacks up on the 5pt. If we were to shift Black's spare on his 5pt to his 6pt, then eXtreme Gammon would slightly favor 16/13 7/5 over 7/2, even at this score (see Variant 2 below).
