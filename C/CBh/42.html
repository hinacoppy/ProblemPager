Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=121 White=146
This is an area of the game where the bots have considerably improved our play over the last ten years.

Red has the dual objectives of closing out White and getting the back checkers moving. 13/10, 13/9 concentrates too much on the former while something like 20/13 is not aggressive enough.

Many would vote for 21/14 which is reasonable but best of all is 20/16, 13/10 (!!) which meets both objectives outlined above. The play may look very loose but unless White rolls 44 any other hitting number leaves multiple return shots and therefore potential gammon wins for Red.

Once you see the play it seems clear but many would not have it as a candidate play. Hopefully now you have seen the technique you can include it in your backgammon armoury.
