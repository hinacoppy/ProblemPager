pips Red=115 White=110

Red is the underdog in this position. Should he choose a slow death or is it time to go over the top? The position is akin to being in a First World War trench. If Red plays 8/2, 6/1 or 13/7, 13/8 he will be hoping for a miracle to save him.

Better to bite the bullet (metaphorically) now and play 22/16, 6/1. Yes, White may hit but he is unlikely to be able to cover his blot on his 2-pt and if Red hits it he will be well on the way to victory. He might re-anchor on the 22-pt and then he's not much worse off than he was before.

If instead he elects for a holding play then White' position will improve and when Red is subsequently forced off the anchor a hit may well prove terminal.

There are lots of ifs and maybes but analysis shows that Red must go over the top now and hope for the best - any other move is a blunder.
