Money Play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=126 White=131
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Two of the threes are pretty much forced – bar/22 and 10/7*. What about the other two? Leaving five blots with either 5/2(2) or 6/3(2) looks way too risky and it destroys Red’s board forever. After the board is gone it’s difficult to see Red winning – he needs to re-establish the fifth home board point if possible.

Therefore the choice is between bar/22, 10/7(2)*, 5/2 and bar/22, 10/7(2)*, 6/3.

Surprisingly the rollouts have any play other than bar/22, 10/7(2)*, 5/2 as a blunder! It would seem that the duplication of 5’s after that play and the fact with the other play White hits twice with much more purity is enough to swing a couple of percentage points to White.

In all honesty I was very surprised at the rollout and it took me some time to think through why one play was so much better than the other but I’m happy I now understand although the equity difference still seems high!
