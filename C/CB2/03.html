The Trice adjusted Ward Count gives White's point of Last Take as 61 if this was a money game. Allowing about 2% for each extra pip then White's winning chances are approximately 13% (in fact XG has them at 14.5%).

His take point (0-6 to 7 Crawford) is a mere 8% so this is a trivial take and the question is the redouble. In fact the redouble is in triple blunder country and Red should wait.

In Sean's defence he correctly calculated Ray's winning chances but somehow got his Crawford equities mixed up and therefore redoubled thinking Ray was close to his take point. Ray redoubled to 8 on his next roll but there was no excitement for the crowds and Sean won easily.
