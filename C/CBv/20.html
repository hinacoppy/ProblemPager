Match Play. Red leads 2-1 to 7. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=140 White=118
This is not the time to give up the anchor by hitting with 21/16*, tempting though that might be. One return hit from White and Red could easily lose a gammon. Making the 3-pt with 6/3, 5/3 is correct by a long way and any other move is a blunder or worse.

Also, Red owns the cube so he can build towards an effective redouble if that becomes necessary. In a lot of scenarios Red will play on for a gammon but owning the cube is very powerful in positions like this one.
