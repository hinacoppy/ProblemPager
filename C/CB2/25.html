Match Play. DMP. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=75 White=123
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Bill Davis continues to pose excellent and challenging problems on the ABT Facebook account.

In a money game (particularly with White owning the cube on 2) the decision would be easier because of the gammon wins and hitting White's blot on Red's 4-pt would be mandatory. The best way to hit is 8/4*, 5/4 as there is some duplication of 3's. This superior to 8/4*/3 because of the additional cover numbers for Red on the next roll.

At DMP Gammons don't matter either way so you want the play that wins the most games. This brings 13/8 into contention because it starts to clear the most difficult point.

Calculating this over the board is virtually impossible so you have to go by experience and a little bit of gut feel. Back to that old adage –“when in doubt, hit.” It is remarkable just how often the adage is correct.

It turns out that 8/4*, 5/4 wins 1.3% more games than 13/8 so that is the right play.
