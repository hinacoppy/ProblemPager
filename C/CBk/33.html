Match Play. Red trails 1-2 to 5. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=138 White=172
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is not a difficult problem and I posted it more as a reminder than anything else. One of the most common match play errors that I see is players cashing a single point when they are too good and should be playing on for the gammon.

This position is a simple case in point. Red should play on for the gammon here. It is a blunder to simply take the point. Occasionally the plan will backfire but more often Red will win the gammon and after all, backgammon is all largely about percentages.

Of course, if you think your opponent might take this position then the decision is more complex but that will be down to your own knowledge. Against most people I would play on here.
