Money Play. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=81 White=70
This is a useful reference position. Put two of White’s checkers from his ace-point onto his 5-pt, giving him a five-point board and Red must 6/5, 6/2 with this 41.

However, with two open points Red can keep his own five-point home board despite leaving two blots with this 41. The best move is 13/12, 13/9 closely followed by 13/8.

Breaking the 10-pt which blocks White’s sixes is worse than 6/5, 6/2, despite the fact that it leaves only one blot.

13/12, 13/9 is difficult to find, especially over the board under pressure, but it is the right play. White can redouble if he hits and Red fans but Red still has a comfortable take.
