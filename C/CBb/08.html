2016 - Position 65

Money Play. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=155 White=144
Another very instructive position. It might seem that Red should split the back checkers in an attempt to gain a high anchor. However, White has ten checkers in the attack zone, a better home board and his own high anchor so a hitting battle without the security of an anchor is not the right idea.

The correct plan is to try to build a better home board before leaving the rear anchor. Those ten checkers on the 8-pt and 6-pt are crying out for development and so 8/4, 6/5 is the correct play, giving Red a two-way game plan. If hit, he can fall into a back game or holding game. If not hit, he can establish a priming/blocking game. Once he has a stronger home board it will be safer to leave to leave the rear anchor.

All plays other than 8/4, 6/5 are errors and something like 24/23, 6/2 is virtually a blunder.
