Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=108 White=166
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
To quote Kent Goulding: “Four is more than you think”.

White’s four-point prime has great value and holding Red’s ace-point means he is in the game to the end. If Red escapes a rear checker and White fans again then the position becomes a pass but there are many other scenarios in this position.

In fact, it is more the double than the take that is in question. The gammon threat does mean that Red should double but dropping here would be a huge blunder.
