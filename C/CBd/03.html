Apologies from me. This is clearly a huge redouble/pass. Sometimes I take positions from match play and turn them into money game problems but that didn’t work very well here!

In the actual position 23/18, 10/7 is just a shade ahead of 23/18, 6/3*. The latter move loses just a few too many gammons to make it correct and Red can still win with a judiciously timed redouble.

If, however, the cube was on White’s side of the board (which logically it should be) then Red can only win with the checkers and now 23/18, 6/3* just edges out the safer play although it is very close.
