Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=117 White=122
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
We can quickly eliminate 7/4, 7/2. This puts checkers in the wrong position and what is Red going to next turn when White may well have a five-point home board? The choice is to break either the mid-point or the bar-point.

18/15, 18/13 is great when it works but when it doesn’t thing can go wrong very quickly. The correct play is 13/18, 13/10 which slightly reduces the number of hit and cover rolls but importantly keeps the bar-point as an escape route and also maintains the pressure on White’s three checkers on his mid-point.

Both 7/4, 7/2 and 18/13, 18/15 are very close to blunders in XG terms.
