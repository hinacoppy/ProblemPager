Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=151 White=131
Anything involving splitting the back checkers is clearly wrong, giving White good big numbers and good little numbers.

11/7, 9/6 doesn’t really do much to improve Red’s position and gives White free licence.

Many would try 9/2*, keeping White off balance but with only one cover number, even if White fans, it’s not ideal.

At the table 13/10, 13/9 was tried but with 3 blots and 22 shots it’s not surprising that is nearly a double blunder. Correct is following the old age: “put them where you want them” and moving 13/9, 11/8. This creates a five and a half-point prime and although it leaves 20 shots there is only one blot.

Any other move is an error and all bar 9/2* are blunders or worse.
