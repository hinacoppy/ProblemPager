pips Red=83 White=74
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I have a rule of thumb that says that giving up one home board point in positions like this is OK but think carefully before giving up a second point.

This is because Red needs to be able to contain any White checker that is hit and that becomes progressively more difficult as the home board weakens.

Red does much better here to play 13/8 and keep the home board, rather than dismantle it 5/4, 5/1 (which make rolls like 41 and 42 very awkward next turn). Even if the blot is hit he will re-enter immediately 30% of the time and still be in the game.

5/4, 5/1 gives up 5% game winning chances and is nearly a double blunder.
