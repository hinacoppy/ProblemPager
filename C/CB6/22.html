Those six checkers on Red's 6-pt look very ugly and plays like bar/23, 24/23 or bar/24, 13/11 or bar/24, 6/4 don't improve matters much.

This is a case of putting them where you want them' and the correct play is bar/23, 6/5. This keeps the back checkers diversified and starts the most important point on the board.

Any other play is a blunder.
