The key here is to keep a five-point block, including blocking Whites sixes.

7/1(2) is the wrong idea as it reduces the prime and puts two checkers out of play, despite the fact that it makes a five-point home board.

There is virtually no difference between 11/8(2), 7/4(2) and 11/8(2), 4/1(2). The former gets the nod by 0.005.

11/5(2), stacking the 5-pt is inefficient use of the checkers and lets White escape a checker with all sixes.
