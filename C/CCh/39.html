Red’s structure is very impure with the gap on the 4-pt, the dead checker on the ace-point and the blot on the 2-pt. That gap will create plenty of blot-leaving numbers as Red bears in.
On the plus side Red has a fifteen-pip lead in the race and White’s board may crash on the next roll.
All these factors contribute to Red having a double, but only just. White has a trivial take, dropping would be a huge blunder.
