This is difficult because Red has a number of objectives and it is not clear which takes priority.

Historically I would have run off the anchor here and 20/16(2) would have been part of my move but I have learnt to keep anchors for much longer than I used to.

Red can block White’s 6’s from the 3-pt with 13/9(2) and he should do so. He should also play 6/2 making a five point home board. This makes the final four fairly obvious, namely 18/14. This gives Red a flexible position for next turn when he Any other play is at least an error and 20/16(2), 13/9(2) is a blunder. Keep that anchor!
