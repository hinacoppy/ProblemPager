Money Play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=140 White=131
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Small doubles are hardly ever easy to play because of the myriad of choices. Here there are at least six sensible choices. 13/7(2) is reasonable and better than 11/5(2) because the former is easier to improve than the latter.

To make the right choice Red must look at all parts of the board and also the think about the cube. 11/5, 8/5(2) is the second best play but the clear winner is 13/7, 8/5(2).

This takes tactical advantage of White's weak home board and the blot on his 2-pt. It also leads to some potentially very strong doubles. At first sight the play is not obvious but the more you look at it the more obvious it becomes.
