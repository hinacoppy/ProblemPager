Match Play. Red leads 7-4 to 9. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=129 White=107
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Clearly the six is 13/7*.
Equally clearly Red cannot afford to play 4/3 with the ace. He has too much work to do to win the position. The 4-pt must remain slotted.
As White has fourteen checkers in his attack zone Red should not split his rear checkers and give White counter-attacking possibilities. It is much better to resolve the forward position, i.e. make the 4-pt, before moving those rear checkers.
The correct move, therefore, is: 13/12, 13/7*. Note that 13/7*, 5/4 is also perfectly OK.
