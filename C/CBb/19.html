Money Play. How should Red play 62?
pips Red=60 White=151
This is effectively a ‘pay me now, pay me later’ problem.

Red will undoubtedly leave shots as he bears off unless he is very lucky indeed.

Once he has cleared his 6-pt his shot-leaving potential reduces considerably. He does better to leave a shot now while White’s home board is incomplete and has an in-board blot.

6/off, 6/4 wins more games and more gammons so 6/off, 5/3 (which leaves the possibility of leaving triple or double shots next turn) is an error and very nearly a blunder.

It’s always hard to leave voluntary shots but this one is crystal-clear.
