Money play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=213 White=138
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Another difficult problem and one probably worthy of a longer article in its own right.

Red is well-timed for a back game but should at least consider going forward with bar/22, 8/7*. This turns out to be the second-best play. He doesn't have the timing to hold three points in White's board with bar/24, 8/5.

He should hold two points and seek escape options for the other two rear checkers so that he doesn't crash his forward position. The play to do all this is bar/22, 23/22. Any other play but bar/22, 8/7* (which also gives Red flexibility) is at least an error.
