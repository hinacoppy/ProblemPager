Money Play. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=142 White=116
This is a classic ‘put them where you want them’ position.

Anything other than 13/7, 6/5 is at least a double blunder – it is just so hard to win after any of the other plays. White is only a marginal favourite to hit either blot while Red has two re-entry points and holds White’s bar.

If White hits the game is lost if Red fans but if White doesn’t hit then suddenly Red has a very powerful position. Backgammon is a game of risk and reward and that is ably demonstrated by this position.
