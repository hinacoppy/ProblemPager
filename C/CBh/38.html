Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=81 White=72
This came up at the Battersea chouette and Red thought long and hard about doubling. In the end he didn’t, fanned and got doubled out next turn.

Obviously the only reason Red might double is the number of gammons he wins.

I put this into XG and was delighted to find this is a genuine example of the Kauder Paradox. Red should double to reduce his negative equity and White should therefore Beaver!

For a description of the Paradox and more examples see Raymond Kershaw’s really excellent book, “Backgammon Funfair”.
