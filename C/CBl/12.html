The Double Match Point score is a bit of a red herring because it is the same play for DMP or a simple money game.

All of Red’s checkers are well placed except the rear checker. Red also leads in the race. This implies that despite the risk 24/20 is the correct 4. After that there is a choice between 11/8 and 8/5. 8/5 neatly duplicates White’s 1’s and puts a checker where it want to go as opposed to 11/8 which dismantles a useful point. No contest really.

Over the board 8/1 was tried but that “do nothing” play is a blunder. Now is the time to act. 24/20, 8/5 it is.
