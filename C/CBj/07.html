Money play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=122 White=118
This all about containment and connectivity. The wrong idea completely would be 11/5, 7/4(2) which strands the rear checkers and gives White's rear checkers an escape route. 20/17(2), 7/4(2) is also incorrect for much the same reasons.

The choice is really between 11/2, 5/2 making the fifth point of the broken prime, and 20/14(2) which plays to the maxim of racing when ahead and keeps plenty of Red firepower aimed at the 4-pt. The fifth point is good but not as good as maintaining connectivity between the checkers and solving the problem of escaping the rear checkers.

11/2, 5/2 is an error (just) and 20/14(2) wins today's prize.
