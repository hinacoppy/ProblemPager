Money Play. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=123 White=88
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
White’s position is busted. He has poor connectivity, two dead checkers and a damaged home board. Those two checkers on Red’s 9-pt could soon be separated and that leads to gammon losses. In fact, White loses 75% of the time and 25% of those are gammons.

This is a monster redouble and a huge pass – it is nearly a triple blunder to take.

As soon as I have dead checkers and positions where some of my numbers may be forced I look very carefully when doubled and try to estimate the gammon chances for my opponent. In my youth I would take positions like this but with age comes wisdom – nowadays I would drop this very quickly. Note that Red is not too good to redouble.
