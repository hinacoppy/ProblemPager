pips Red=111 White=178

This position came up in USBGF Members vs. Neil Kazaross. I, like many others, liked the idea of securing the 4-pt now with 9/4, 6/4.

However, it turns out that leaving two blots exposed when White has the start of a strong home board is just that little bit too risky and the prosaic 17/10, playing with a single blot, is the correct approach. Making the 4-pt is an error (0.043) but not a blunder.

I certainly learnt something from this position and maybe I won't be so cavalier about leaving blots in future!
