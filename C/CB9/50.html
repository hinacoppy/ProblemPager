Money Play. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=152 White=190
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
As Mochy said when he posted this position you have think this through rather than rely on simple reference positions.

It is often said that any 3-pt game is a take but you don’t often have six checkers back.

This must be a redouble as Red can take command of the outer boards and extend his prime in due course. From White’s perspective Red has a static four-point prime and two dead checkers on his ace-point. The problem is that White needs to quickly escape one of those rear checkers or his forward position could collapse (33 followed by 44 doesn’t look too pretty).

It turns out that White loses too many gammons for this to be a take. Taking is an error but not a blunder (0.048 equity loss).
