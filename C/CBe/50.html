Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=121 White=146
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
A position that the bots have taught us to play. A player from the 1970s would never have found 20/16, 13/10!! exposing five blots.

The key to the play is understanding that the blots protect each other. Any hit by White, other than with 44, leaves numerous return shots for Red. Meanwhile if White fans then Red has got his rear checkers moving and started to bring builders for the open 4-pt.

The more prosaic 21/14 is a close second but the bots have taught us that it is acceptable to take risks when your opponent is on the bar against a five-point home board. Obvious when you think about but not always so easy to put into practice.
