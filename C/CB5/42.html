This is a contender for my position of the year. I had this position at DMP and played the â€˜obvious' 8/7*, 8/3. After 13 by my opponent I rolled 65 (7/2, 6/off), my opponent hit and won the match.

What bad luck I though but then I put it through XG. At DMP 8/7*, 8/3 is a 0.120 blunder!! The empty 2-pt will give Red long-term problems plus after my play most sixes next time leave a shot.

8/7*, 8/3 leaves six shots anyway so is there a better move? Indeed there is: 8/2!! At the cost of five immediate shots Red solves most of his later problems and of course the play duplicates ones. 8/2 wins 6% more games than 8/7*, 8/3.

So for money 8/7*, 8/3 must be right for the gammons? Not at all! Now 8/7*/2 is correct for many of the same reasons as above. Now try convincing your chouette partners! In a doubles tournament could you convince your partner of the correctness of 8/2?
