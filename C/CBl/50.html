Money Play. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=165 White=154
This follows on from the theme of Position 79. Where should Red leave his blot(s)?

One could argue that the bar-point is a key point to make and so 13/8, 13/7 is certainly possible. If Red wants to maintain his mid-point then he can try the single slot 10/5, 8/2 or the double slot (of better points) 10/4, 8/3.

Giving up the mid-point is wrong because it strands the three rear checkers. 13/8, 13/7 is virtually a double blunder.

Because of White's weak home board Red is correct (just) to venture the double slot of two key points, although the single slot play is not an error. The key learning point is not to give up the mid-point in positions like this.
