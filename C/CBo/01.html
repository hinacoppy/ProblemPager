Money Play. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=128 White=67
This is a trade-off between games and gammons. Red can play 14/8, 13/9 paying off to a 6 by White but slotting the point he most wants to make and adding an attacker for White's blot on his 2-pt.

The alternative is 14/10, 13/7 which wins fewer games but loses fewer gammons.

A roll-out has the two plays neck and neck with 14/8, 13/9 just winning by a short head.

Hopefully nobody wanted to play 24/14 which dismantles Red's final anchor.

As is often the case the aggressive play wins out.
