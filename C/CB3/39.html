Money Game. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=146 White=124
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The problem for White here is the high percentage of gammons that he loses when things go wrong. He has the blot on his 1-pt to worry about and the disjointed nature of his position. If you take a checker from White's 6-pt and put it on his 1-pt, thus giving him a two-point home board and no additional blots then the position is a clear no double/take.

However, in the actual position it would be a double blunder by Red not to double. The position is volatile and those gammons loom large. In most instances hit/fan loses Red's market by a country mile. Apply Woolsey's Law – are you 100% sure White has a take? If not – double. White has an easy take.

Also the disjointed nature of White's position means he won't be able to redouble for some time even if things go well for him
