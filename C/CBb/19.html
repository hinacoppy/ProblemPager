I posted this one because someone (who will remain nameless) in the Battersea chouette played 13/4* to maximise his gammon chances.

This is an error (but not a blunder) because the downside far outweighs the upside. Offering a gratuitous shot when your opponent has a very strong home board is rarely right na dthis position is no exception.

Simply 13/7, 8/5 is best. Move White’s blot on his 11-pt to his ace-point and now hitting is correct as White’s timing is more compromised. The break-even point is if that checker on the 11-pt is on the 6-pt. Even in this simple position there are still lessons to be learnt.
