Money Play. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=142 White=95
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I was caught out by this one as I played 18/13, 8/4. This leaves 8 shots and when my opponent rolled 62 I quickly lost a gammon.

Red is going to have to take some risks to turn this game round so playing completely safe with 8/4, 6/1 is not the right idea – the problems will only repeat themselves over subsequent moves. Similarly running off the anchor must be discounted – that way leads to 61% gammon losses!

The correct move is 18/14, 8/3*. This leaves 13 shots and 3 blots as opposed to 8shots and 2 blots but it gains hugely on White’s nine fanning numbers and crucially it might enable Red to gibe White a difficult cube decision in the not too distant future.

18/14, 8/3* wins 4% more games than 18/13, 8/4 while only losing 5% more gammons so that is good value for money.

It is difficult to take risks when your opponent has a five-point home board but backgammon is all about risk and reward and here the risks are justified in order to try to gain the ascendancy.
