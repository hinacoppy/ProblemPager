Red's position is parlous indeed but with the right play he will win nearly 24% of the time. Sadly he will lose a gammon nearly 39% of the time and 43% if he chooses the play made over the board, bar/24, 6/2.

Red can win by anchoring and hitting a late shot or he can win by counter-priming White. He can also win by creating a back game. Bar/24, 13/9 puts no pressure on White but bar/24, 8/4* does. Now Red can quickly build a prime or revert to the anchoring/back game plans if hit.

Anything other than bar/24, 8/4* is an error at best.
