Match Play. Red leads 6-4 to 7 (post-Crawford). How should Red play 51?
pips Red=129 White=112
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The bottom line to this position is that the match score does not influence the correct play. For money or at any match score, even leading 5-3 to 7, 13/7 is the correct play.

To win the game Red will very likely need his bar-point and he doesn’t make it very often after 13/12, 13/8. He must take the risk of slotting with 13/7. Yes, he loses 5% more gammon but he wins 8% more games so it’s really a no-brainer.

Remember the old backgammon adage: “Put them where you want them”!!
