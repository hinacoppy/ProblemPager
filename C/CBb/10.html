2016 - Position “The Independent Finale”

Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=92 White=26
The Independent Column started in 1994 at which point this problem would have been insoluble. Manual rollouts just wouldn’t have been good enough to determine the correct play with acceptable accuracy.

Red needs to pick up the second checker to win the game so closing the board with 12/6 is a long way wrong. He could play 13/12 with the 1 to block White’s 66 but then leave the 6-pt slotted by playing the 6 moving 23/17.

The correct solution, only determined by a long rollout, is to play 12/6, 2/1!! This gives White the chance to hit a blot so that Red can attempt to pick up the White’s blot on his ace-point while at the same time making it as difficult as possible for White to escape Red’s home board.

I would wager that in 1994 nobody would have found this play over the board. In 2016 we have the luxury of expert assistance from XG so we can both find and understand the answer. This technique can then be applied to similar problems in the future. Thus is progress made.
