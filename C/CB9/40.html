pips Red=130 White=118

An interesting position and one that is very easy to get wrong over the board, as I did, by playing 24/14 which is nearly a double blunder.

Red trails in the race, even after playing his move. 24/14 gives White an attack both in Red’s home board and in White’s home board. Nearly all White’s numbers play well.

8/2, 6/2 is merely a blunder making the wrong home board point.

Much better, and the only play that gives Red positive equity is 8/4, 8/2 which makes a stronger four-point prime and severely restricts White’s good numbers.

In essence 24/14 leaves White vulnerable on both sides of the board. As I have often stressed you should only fight a war on one front. Sadly I didn’t manage to obey my own dictates on this occasion!
