Money Play. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=169 White=165
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is a difficult problem. Killing a checker with the 4 is not the right idea so we have the choice of 23/18; 22/18, 6/5 and 24/20, 23/22. The plays are very close and none of them would be classified as an error. Note however that 24/20, 6/5 would be an error. This is because you don’t want to be hit twice and let your opponent start a new home board point at the same time – It’s much better to have both hits outside the opponent’s home board.

XG has a mild preference for the very pure 22/18, 6/5. This strives to make White’s bar-point or at least promote an exchange of hits there. It also unstacks the heavy 6-pt. I’d be happy with any of the plays in a chouette and the anchoring play comes out second best in the rollouts.

Partly this is a matter of style and you should play these positions in a way that you feel comfortable with.
