The key to this position is to limit the number of Red blots to one.

23/16 follows the game plan of running for home when ahead but if one blot is hit the other becomes a liability.

The only move that is not a blunder is 23/18, 13/11. The 11-pt is very useful when Red has an open 5-pt. Meanwhile the rear blot starts its journey for home and Red can concentrate his efforts on that.
