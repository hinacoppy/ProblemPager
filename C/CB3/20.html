Position submitted by George Jacobs

What a deceptive position!

Despite the 32 shots this is nowhere near a double for money. Red will never be able to afford to hit loose once White enters so must rely on making points naturally. In addition any fly shot that White hits from the bar will be an immediate winner. It is nearly a double blunder to offer the cube in a money game.

At this match score the key thing is estimating gammons. If Red closes out both White checkers he will a gammon 40% of the time but because of the constraints already noted that isn't going to happen very often. Before rolling it out I estimated 10% gammons but even that turns out to be too high. The actual figure is just over 8%. White will win this position around 38% of the time. Those fly shots add up plus White can anchor and win with a late shot or by winning the race.

If you plug the percentages above into a match equity chart you find that the position is a clear double and a huge take. Dropping is worse than a quintuple blunder!!
