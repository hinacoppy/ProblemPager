Thank you for all the comments. I think over the board most would elect to play 18/14, 18/13 leaving only five shots as opposed to the eleven left after 18/9.

But then you notice that White’s 14 is awkward. He will still hit 8/7*/3 to take advantage of his superior board but he leaves a direct shot. Then you add in that 18/9 is the better play if White rolls virtually any six and you begin to wonder.

18/9 maintains better pressure on White’s outer boards and in fact 18/14, 18/13 is a racing play and Red is still 16 pips behind after the roll.

The direct shot left after 18/9 would influence many to choose 18/14, 18/13 but in fact that play is nearly a blunder and 18/9 is right by a long way for all the reasons above. It goes to show that immediate shots are not the only thing you should consider when evaluating a position.
