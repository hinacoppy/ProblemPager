A pathetic collective failure by some of the UK's top players (including yours truly) on this one from Battersea.

Given it as quiz problem it is not hard to find 11/7*, 8/2. This wins 5% more games and 17% (!!) more gammons than 8/4, 8/2 which is a quadruple blunder.

The team (Red) were fearful of leaving three blots but even if White does hit one of them and White fans (about a 15% chance) White doesn't have a redouble. Meanwhile if White fans the gammon looms large.

In the game from which this was taken, White remade his 6-pt and then Red left two shots while clearing the 11-pt and 9-pt. The race was also close but Red just crept home. All of this could have been avoided by playing 11/7*, 8/2 in the first place.

Give White his 6-pt by moving the spare checker from his 1-pt and it is a different story altogether. Now 11/7*, 8/2 is too dangerous and 8/4, 8/2 is clear!
