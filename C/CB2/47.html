Money Play. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=100 White=71
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is an object lesson in putting the checkers where you want them and optimising their usage.

Over the board I played 6/1 but that turns out to be a blunder. Red doesn't need to keep both checkers on the 8-pt and one of them should be put to work with 8/5. After that, 6/4 looks the natural play but where does that spare checker on the 3-pt (known as a dilly builder) belong? Obviously on the 1-pt so why not put it there now? A five-point board should be enough to win the game after a hit and here optimising the use of the checkers outweighs the purity of trying to make the 4-pt.

It is close and 8/5, 6/4 is not an error but 8/5, 3/1 is the most accurate play.
