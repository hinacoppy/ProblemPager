pips Red=155 White=171
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
It’s easy to go wrong here. Making the 4-pt with 8/4(2) looks obvious but you need to think about the back checkers as well – they could easily become trapped.

So maybe 23/21(2), 10/6? No that’s too weak and hard to develop. Perhaps not obvious but the correct play is 23/21, 8/4, 6/4, Making the 4-pt asset but also advancing the rear checkers while White has only 9 checkers in the attack zone.

Very few students find the right play this position. In fact any play other than 23/21, 8/4, 6/4 is a blunder.
