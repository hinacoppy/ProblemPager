Match Play. Red trails 2-3 to 5. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=148 White=185
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Yet another demonstration of the power of the match score in short matches.

For money this is no double because of White’s anchor on the 2-pt. However, at 3-away, 2-away any threat of gammon is normally enough to turn the cube.

In this position it would be a double blunder not to turn the cube and White has only a bare take.

If your thinking was to hold the cube because 2 points give White the match then think again – 4 points gives Red the match and a measly 2 points gets him to Crawford!
