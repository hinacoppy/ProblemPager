Money Game. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=62 White=35
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Given this as a quiz problem most people will find 9/6, 1/off giving White the opportunity to roll 21! The question is whether you would find it over the board where it is oh so easy to play 9/5.

9/5 is closer to a blunder than an error. The position demonstrates just how easy it is to take your eye off the ball and play on automatic. To play winning backgammon you have to squeeze the last drop of equity out of each and every position.

This position is a classic demonstration of that principle.
