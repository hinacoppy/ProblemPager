Money Play. How should Red play 66?
pips Red=151 White=174
There are many different ways to play this 66 and I could analyse each of them but the blitz is an area of the game where the blots have vastly increased our knowledge in the last twenty years.

Their fundamental teaching for such is positions is simply this: ‘don't give your opponent the chance to anchor unless you have to'.

This implies that the correct move is 20/2*, 8/2. Yes – it leaves two home board blots but a three can tidy those up and the blot on the 4-pt can be covered by a nine next time. Twelves also play well.

In my youth I would never have played 20/2*, 8/2 and would probably never have even considered it. I had this position over the board and am pleased to say I swiftly played 20/2*, 8/2 and won a gammon. Any other play is a blunder giving away at least 0.098 in equity!

If this position helps you play your blitzes better in future then it has served its purpose.
