Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=142 White=165
Red's four-point prime (with the potential for becoming a five-point prime) is stronger than it looks. To quote Kent Goulding: “Four is more than you think”.

This coupled with White's inflexible structure including that stack of checkers on the 8-pt and the blot on his ace-point means that White has to pass Red's double.

Taking is an error but not a blunder.
