Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=127 White=80
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
These always look better for the attacking side than they actually are.

White sees only a six from Red and then his board collapsing and the loss of a gammon.

But White leads by 47 pips in the race and has, at least for the moment, a stronger home board and he has a five-point prime. All of this adds up to a very comfortable take for White.

Still a very strong double for Red of course because a six does give him real gammon chances and he has his own five-point prime.

Learning to take positions like this one as White is a key to winning backgammon.
