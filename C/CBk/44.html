Run out with 21/13 and keep a perfect board or wait a roll with 6/3, 6/1?

This is very close call. With the race very close it turns out to be better by a very small amount of equity to hold the anchor. My rule of thumb for such positions is that I don’t mind giving up one home board point but I don’t want to come down to having only a four-point home board.

That rule would work here but as noted above, only just. Note that if it was White who held the cube then 21/13 would be correct by quite a long way as in that variation Red must win with the checkers and has no cube leverage. Therefore, he should not give up his perfect board. Overall Red will win from here about one third of the time.
