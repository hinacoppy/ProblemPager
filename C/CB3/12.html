Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=134 White=151
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Good to see a nice healthy debate on this one from the regular contributors - many thanks to all of you.

This is cross between a blitz and a prime vs. prime but more often the latter.

White's home board position is very powerful and will lead to him winning a lot of games when Red's blitz fails or Red can't escape his rear checkers.

However, Red is on roll and attacking and will win a fair number of gammons (more than half his wins are gammons). The position is highly volatile so Red should double now (not doubling is not quite a blunder). Equally well White has a lot of ways to win and has a very easy take (worse than a quadruple blunder to pass!). Note that 25% of White's wins will be gammons.

First appearances can be deceptive and Red's position looks more dangerous than it is. He will attack with any hitting number (31 must be played 6/2*) but if White return hits he may well turn the position around very quickly.
