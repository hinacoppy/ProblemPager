Match Play. Red leads 3-2 to 5. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=118 White=166
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red has four stripped outer board points and has just been given the chance to clear one of them and create new builders - he should take it.
White has an anchor, and the cube is in the middle and both of these point to clearing the 14-pt.
While winning a gammon is very powerful, the first priority is to win the game. If Red plays 9/3*, 8/3 and White enters Red will still have a lot of work to do.
Connectivity implies that 14/9, 14/8 is the right way to go and indeed it is. Making the 3-pt is a bad blunder.
