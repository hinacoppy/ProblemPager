Black is significantly behind in the pip count and would therefore like to contain White's remaining straggler.
In particular, Black would like to prevent White from escaping with a 6.
White's weak board gives Black license to play the bold 13/9 6/5, which works very well if White does not roll a 6.
If White's 5pt blot were covered (see Variant position below), then 13/9 6/5 would still be a candidate, but so would the prosaic 6/5 6/2.
The equity gap between the 1st and 2nd plays in the rollout is surprisingly large for such an early-game play.
