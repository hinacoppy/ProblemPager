Money Play. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=158 White=96
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red cannot hold both his back game and his forward game. Over the board he tried 8/2*, 7/2 but the chances of containing a single checker with an imperfect home board are very low and that is the wrong game plan.

Red should recognise that he must give up the forward anchor and play 20/9 with the idea of priming White’s rear checker. 20/9 gives very real duplication of 2’s and even 26.

Owning the cube he retains the long-term asset of the 3-point anchor and the chance to win from there if White escapes the rear checker. In fact 20/9 makes Red a 58% favourite to win the game.
