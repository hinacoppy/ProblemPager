Double Match Point. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=80 White=87
This one is quite straightforward.

If Red plays 7/6/3 he will be ahead by 10 pips and a reasonable favourite â€“ in fact he will be about 70% to win the match.

If he plays 7/4*/3 he will be hit on eleven rolls. 31 is very good for White but on his other hits he still has to escape Red's board by rolling a 3 and then a 5 or a 6. On White's sixteen fanning rolls Red gains hugely.

Overall the hit gains far more than it loses and increases Red's winning chances by about 4% - huge in backgammon terms.
