Everything about this position demands an attacking play from Red.

He has three checkers behind a broken prime (that theme again). He has the better home board and White has a home board blot.

This is no time for halfway measures with something like 13/3*. The only viable plan is a blitz/counter-prime and so the logical play must be 9/5*, 9/3*. This play gives Red the option of going forwards or, if things go badly, falling into a back game.

Any play other than the double hit is a double blunder or worse.
