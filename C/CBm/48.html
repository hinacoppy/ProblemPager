Money Game. How should Red play 51?
pips Red=130 White=107
This is very difficult as evidenced by the fact that nobody in our chouette selected the best play. Nothing looks much good. The key decision is whether to play against the single checker by attacking it with 8/7, 6/1*, play as safe as you can with something like 8/2 or to play the position as prime vs. prime.

The problem with the hit is that it creates three blots and therefore gammon losses increase. Meanwhile Red still has two rear checkers to escape with not much time to do so and White will surely escape.

8/2 is anti-thematic – how will Red improve from here? Answer – not very easily. We can discard this idea (or lack of idea).

The correct plan is to play this as prime vs. prime. 8/3, 6/5 is a good try but 8/7, 8/3 is much, much better. But now the key question – can you take the cube after any play?
