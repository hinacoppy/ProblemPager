Money Play. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=136 White=141
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Over the board I was seduced by the prospect of picking up the second White blot and so played bar/24, 3/2(2)*, 6/5.

This is not an unreasonable plan but 3/2(2)* means that Red holds two point six pips apart and that significantly damages his priming chances. Given that he ahead in the race the right plan turns out to be combining racing and priming chances and the correct play is bar/22, 6/5.

It goes to show just how difficult double ones can be to play. Even bar/23, 7/5 is an error as it gives White more attacking chances. In case you wondered bar/24, 7/4 is a double blunder.
