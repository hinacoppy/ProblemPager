pips Red=160 White=158

This position occurs after Red 61: 13/7, 8/7 and White 54: 24/20, 13/8.

I have often seen it played 8/5*/1* but this is the wrong idea, giving up the 8-pt. When your opponent is threatening to make an advanced anchor you can counter by trying to make your own advanced anchor. The correct play is 24/21, 24/20.

24/21, 13/9, triplicating fours, is also reasonable but not quite as strong as advancing both rear checkers.
