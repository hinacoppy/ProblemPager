Money Play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=134 White=125
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
White is threatening too much if left unmolested and Red trails in the race. Note also that White owns the cube so Red can only win with the efficient use of his checkers.

5/2*/1 is the correct play and any other play (e.g. 10/6) is at least a double blunder.

The key here is to see the play as a candidate in the first place and recognise the need for a very tactical play. With one opposing checker back attacking is often the right approach and it certainly is here.
