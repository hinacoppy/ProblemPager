The obvious play here is 13/5, but then most sixes on the next roll will force us to leave a double shot. 6/1 5/2 is safe but ugly.
White's weak board allows us to make the bold, flexible play of 13/8 5/2, duplicating White's ones.
Strengthen White's board (see Variant 1 below) and we have to play 6/1 5/2.
Note that 13/5 may not be correct even if we still have four checkers on the midpoint; see Variant 2 below.
