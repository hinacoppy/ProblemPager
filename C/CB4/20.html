Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=42 White=46
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The point about including what is a ‘simple end game' is that to maximise your long-term winnings you need to get positions like this spot on.

Most players will do some basic calculations, look at the 4-pip lead, worry about the empty 4-pt and then make a decision with many doing the dreaded ‘I'll just take a roll'.

The work done by Ward, Kleinman, Trice and others shouldn't be ignored and you can find the necessary details in the ‘Backgammon Races' monograph in the download section of this website.

This position is a double (just) and a very easy take. You potentially give up 0.023 equity points by not doubling. In the great scheme of things that is not a lot but if you consistently give up such amounts they eventually add up. So my suggestion is that you learn to apply Ward/Trice – you'll be happier and potentially richer!
