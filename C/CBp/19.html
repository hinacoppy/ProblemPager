Money Play. How should Red play 51?
pips Red=88 White=84
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red needs his checkers to work to bring home the position.

If he plays 6/1, 3/2 his problems will increase because he won't have a five-point home board when he attacks White, as we will surely have to do. Even the compromise play of 13/12, 6/1 isn't best. It leaves 6 shots and kills a checker.

The choice is between 13/17 and 13/8, 9/8. The latter is much easier to clean up if the blot is missed. The two plays are close but 13/8, 9/8 is best. 13/7 is a mistake but not an error (in XG terms).

Note that irrespective of the position of the cube 13/8, 9/8 is the best play.
