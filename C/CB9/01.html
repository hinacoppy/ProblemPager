Money Play. How should Red play 63?
pips Red=122 White=67
It is a choice between the quiet 15/9, 6/3 creating a powerful array of attacking builders if White doesn’t roll a 6, or the aggressive 15/9, 7/4* when White has to hit and then leap the prime (or just leap it with 55 or 65).

I am always wary when my opponent has a five-point home board. Give White his bar-point instead of his 5-pt and I would hit in a flash.

My gut instinct was correct here and I played, correctly, 15/9, 6/3. When White didn’t roll a six I successfully closed him out.

The hitting play exchanges an extra 10% gammon losses for only 3% more wins – not a good deal. Here the five-point board does enough to deter the hit. 15/9, 6/3 it is.
