Money Play. How should play 64?
pips Red=162 White=138
The automatic play is 15/9, 13/9 and I wouldn’t argue with a chouette captain who wanted to make that play.

However, Red can do slightly better by playing 22/16, 13/9. There is duplication of 3’s and 4’s and it deflects the action from White’s home board to his outer board which is better for Red. It also escapes a checker from White’s home board and gives additional coverage of Red’s outer board.

Technically 15/9, 13/9 is an error but once again it is understanding the concept that is important so that the technique can be reused.
