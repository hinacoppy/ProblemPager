Match Play. Red leads 5-1 to 9. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=104 White=159
A fascinating position mainly because nearly everybody who commented on this on Facebook after Sean Williams posted it thought it was definitely a take and possibly no double.

The lack of volatility, the gaps in Red's prime and the dead checkers on the ace-point all contributed to this thinking.

XG is more pragmatic. The 55-pip racing deficit plus the fact that White is not yet ready to contain a hit checker both contribute to White losing a lot of gammons. So much so that this is a pass at this score and for money as well.

Sean took, got gammoned and lost the match.
