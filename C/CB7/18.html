pips Red=150 White=147
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Addition Trivia Question: How many times do you need to roll the dice before you become favourite to roll a specific double, e.g. 66? (This came up in relation to Position 17).

I had this position in a match and played 11/1(2)*. My opponent fanned a couple of times and I won an easy gammon.

XG thinks this is an overplay and prefers 13/3(2). This gives much better connectivity between Red’s checkers. White’s home board is just strong enough to discourage a blitz and so the pure play wins this one. White is not really threatening much on his side of the board and 13/3(2) makes a useful point while developing the spare checkers from the mid-point.

XG rates 11/1(2)* as an error but only just and at certain match scores, i.e. when trailing, it could well be the right play.

The answer to the rolls question is 25 (see Alastair's comment for the necessary equaton). A few hundred years ago a french gambler did the maths wrong and came up with 24. That small error lost him a lot of money over the years!
