Money Play. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=149 White=134
The key to this position is to recognise that the blots on the 11- and 10-pts are assets and not liabilities. Behind in the race Red would love the chance to make another point in White’s home board.

Those blots exert pressure on White’s 16-pt. After you understand the strategy it’s not too difficult to find 7/4, 6/4 building the point you want next. Picking up both blots is nearly a blunder. Of course any play involving 22/20 is at least an error.
