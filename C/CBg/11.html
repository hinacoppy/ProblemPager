Match Play. Red trails 3-4 to 5 (Crawford Game). How should Red play 54?
pips Red=96 White=95
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one is fairly straightforward.

For money with a centred cube 6/1, 5/1 would be clear as Red cannot win a gammon. For money with White owning a 2-cube 13/4* just edges out 6/1, 5/1 because of the gammon wins.

At 3-4 to 5 (Crawford) gammons are perfectly efficient for Red and so it is no surprise that 13/4* is the correct play. Now 6/1, 5/1 is nearly a blunder.
