Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=186 White=143
The key here is not to attempt an attack with 8/4*, 74. Red has too few checkers in the attack zone to try to blitz White.

He does much better by keeping his four-point prime on his own side of the board and keeping his options for a variety of back games open on the other side of the board.

There is very little difference between a) 21/14 b) 23/20, 14/10 and c) 23/20, 15/11

The latter wins the rollout by 0.007 but that is incidental. The key learning point is not to mistakenly go for the blitzing game plan.
