Money Play. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=146 White=165
Over the board it is very easy to play 16/0, 3/2 but Red can create better flexibility and maintain his home board by playing 16/9.

White is unlikely to be tempted off Red’s 5-pt to hit and then Red’s checkers will be well place to make the 8-pt or bar-pt next turn.

The key is to see that in reality the blots are not in any real danger and even in the sequences where one or both of them are hit Re will be able to re-enter easily.

16/10, 3/2 and 16/10, 4/3 are both errors but not blunders.
