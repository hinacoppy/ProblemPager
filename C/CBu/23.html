Match Play. 0-0 to 11. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=104 White=140
I keep getting this type of position wrong but this time it worked in my favour.

In a money game it would be a double blunder not to double (activating gammons), while at 0-0 to 11 it would still be a blunder not to double. However, at this score giving the cube away with a redouble is a blunder, because of the cube leverage it gives White for any potential redouble to 8.

With the safety of his ace-point anchor White has many ways to win this game, especially as Red has three of his own blots.

I had this position as Red in the first round at the recent Cotswolds tournament. I redoubled and opponent took a full three minutes before he dropped.

In this case fortune favoured the bold but it is a highly instructive position to help in understanding match play doubling.
