Money Play. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=124 White=139
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I had no idea of the right play when I saw this one. My opponent played 13/12(2), 10/9(2) which is the third best play, apparently.

There is no need to leave a voluntary indirect shot as White can still win by going forward. The play to avoid is 13/11(2) because that point will be hard to clear later. Red should be thinking of clearing points, not making them.

To that end the best play should be 10/8(2) and it is but only by 0.002 equity points! It gives White a horrendous 33 next time! Equally good is 10/9(2), 2/1(2) which combines clearance with blocking. Note the mid-point is not advances as this will leave fewer indirect shots when one checker is move from there later.

This is a difficult problem and I’m sure this short explanation doesn’t cover all the learning points from the position. As I have said before, backgammon is a fiendishly difficult game.
