Match Play. Red trails 0-4 to 7. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=141 White=146
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I had this position against Rachel Rhodes in the Scope tournament last weekend.

For money this is nowhere near a double and it would be a big blunder to do so. At this score however Red can double much earlier than usual with a gammon threat – which Red certainly has here – and White will hardly ever be able to redouble so the cube is virtually dead after Red's double.

According to the rollouts the double is correct by 0.004 so basically either doubling or not doubling is correct – the key is to at least consider doubling.

I did double, rolled an innocuous 65 and some time later lost the race – in fact Rachel was able to redouble me out when she established a huge lead.

I won the Crawford Game but lost the next one and thus the match with it. I fared better in the Consolation which I won beating Tariq Saddiqi 9-8 in a close-fought final.
