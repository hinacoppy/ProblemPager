Money Play. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=128 White=84
The key here is not to give up the 23-pt in order to maintain pressure on the White checkers on his 11-pt.

Red’s plan should be to play a well-timed 2-pt game. The problem with giving up the 23-pt with 23/12 is that triples Red’s gammon losses! It does win a few more games but they don’t compensate for the gammon losses.

At DMP 23/12 would be correct but not in this money game. Either 18/12, 18/13 or 18/7 is OK with virtually nothing between them.
