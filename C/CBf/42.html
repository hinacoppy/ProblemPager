2015 - Position 110

Money Play:a) Should red redouble? If redoubled should White take?
pips Red=26 White=101
b) Move the two checkers on White’s 3-pt to his 2-pt. Should Red redouble? If redoubled should White take?
c) Move the two checkers on White’s 4-pt to his 2-pt. Should Red redouble? If redoubled should White take?


Position submitted by Chris Purchase

A very instructive position: if Red had another checker on his ace-point all three positions would be too good to double and pass because with two checkers on his ace-point and one checker closed out Red has a redouble which White must drop.

The tactical opportunities afforded by the blot on the ace-point changes things in White’s favour. He can slot his open point, get hit, pick up and then close out the second checker.

With the 2-pt open that is good enough to make it no redouble for Red. With the 3-pt open Red can escape more easily and that makes it a redouble and a take. With the 4-pt open the escape is even easier and that makes it too good to redouble/pass.
