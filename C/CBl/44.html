Money Play. How should Red play 44?
pips Red=127 White=170
White's timing is precarious so Red should not improve it by hitting the blot on the 17-pt although this would be many players first instinct. White's board is not strong enough to make hitting a necessity.

After bar/21 making the 4-pt is the natural play with two more fours becauset hat is the point Red most wants to make.

The fourth four could be played 9/5 but making the 9-pt is stronger, effectively creating a partial prime in front of White's checkers on Red's 3-pt.

So the correct play is bar/21, 13/9, 8/4(2). Bar/21, 9/5, 8/4(2) is not quite an error but any other play is at least an error.
