Match Play. Red trails 2-3 to 7. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=156 White=152
For money this is not a redouble. Red has too much work to do and the cube is powerful weapon in White’s hands. However, after a redouble here the cube is dead. Not redoubling here is a double blunder.

A single win gets Red to the Crawford Game with his opponent on an odd number and a gammon wins him the match – powerful arguments indeed.

Red must know how to play certain rolls. With 62 and 52, for example, he must be good enough to hit on his bar-point and his 4-pt – the short-term risks are outweighed by the potentially huge gains.
