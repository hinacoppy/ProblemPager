It is easy, especially over the board, to see only two plays here: 16/10* and 21/16 9/8. However, the blot on the 8pt is not in all that much danger (there are some rolls where White will decline to break anchor to hit), and the blot on the 23pt is poorly positioned.
Many players underestimate how weak a blot on the 23pt is when it is blocked by the opponent's 8pt.
Shift Black's blot forward a pip to the 22pt and the decision is much closer, with 21/16 9/8 apparently best (see variant below).
