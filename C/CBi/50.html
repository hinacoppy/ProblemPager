Money play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=113 White=195
A quadruple blunder by my good self on this one.

For some time I had been trying to ensure that White couldn't keep his timing and I continued this plan with bar/24, 13/10. The problem with this play is that I am likely to have to hit another White blot anyway to escape the rear checker.

I should have switched to giving White a super-timed back game by hitting with bar/22*/21* and then hitting more blots. The end result of this strategy is that a) I win many more backgammons and b) the resulting play after a late White hit can be very complex and only the best will navigate those complexities without errors.

This is a good example of knowing when to change strategies and sadly this time I failed the test but I learnt something very useful from this position.
