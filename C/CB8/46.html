This was posted to remind everyone that checkers don’t need to move in pairs when you roll doubles – as happened at the table.

You can play with 17/13(2) or 17/15, 9/7(2) – the second best move – but Red wants to hang back, block sixes and give himself some flexibility. 17/9 does all of that.

Once you see the move it is blindingly obvious – the problem for many is seeing it in the first place.
