Match Play. Red leads 5-1 to 7. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=108 White=81
Red worried about losing a freak gammon here and played the weak 11/4, leaving only one exposed blot.

However, he should just have played ‘normal’ backgammon. The best way to win the game is to close out White and that should start with the correct play of 9/2*. Any other play is at least a blunder.

Many players tighten up when leading and play over-cautiously and this position is a classic example of that.
