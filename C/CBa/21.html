Money Play. How should Red play 44?
pips Red=128 White=115
Now is not the time to leave a shot so the choice is 14/6(2) or 14/10(2), 7/3(2).

The latter has a lot going for it: it maintains connectivity between Red’s checkers; it makes a new home board point and it blocks White’s sixes from Red’s 4-pt.

The only down side is the checker partially out of play on Red’s 3-pt but that is not enough of a problem to vote in favour of 14/6(2).
