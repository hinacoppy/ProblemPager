On initial evaluation XG much prefers hitting on the ace-point with the 5 but the rollout has 17/12, 7/4 virtually dead-heating with 7/4, 6/1*.

After 7/4, 6/1* Red won’t be hitting the other White blot with a 1 because that is needed for 24/23.

The question is whether Red can burrow out of White’s board or, if left to his own devices, will White self-destruct? After 17/12, 7/4 both players have roughly the same amount of spare pips to play with before their boards begin to destruct but Red has two checkers trapped that are not at the edge of the prime while White has one checker at the edge of Red’s prime.

White is a 70-30 favourite in the position. The hitting plays both wins and loses more gammons.

Over the board this is a very difficult position and even analysing after the event it is not a lot easier. I played 17/12, 7/4 which according to XG is a 0.01 error – I can live with that!
