(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
A safe play is the wrong idea here. Red wants to make his bar-point and/or attack the White blot on his ace-point. He needs to get his checkers in play and given White’s poor home board he can play with little fear.
The best move is 18/13, 15/14. 15/9 is OK but it is still a mistake because it leaves three checkers on White’s bar-point and that is inefficient. The checkers must work for a living.
