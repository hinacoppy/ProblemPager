2019 - Position 137

Match Play. 4-4 to 7. How should Red play 22?
pips Red=153 White=137
I had this position as White against one of the top players on GridGammon and was surprised when he played 13/11, 8/4, 6/4. I thought he would play 22/20(2), 9/7(2).

He is not a top player for nothing. His play was absolutely correct My play ties with 13/7, 9/7 but both are bad errors according to XG.

It turns out that creating a five-point broken prime is more important than advancing the rear checkers or making the bar. Red trails in the race after the roll so now I can see the logic of the play.

We have frequently seen the power of the broken prime on this website and this is another position to add to the collection. Note also that having an additional home board point makes it more dangerous for White to flee with one rear checker. 13/11, 8/4, 6/4 wins more gammons than any other play.
