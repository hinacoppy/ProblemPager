Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=110 White=88
Making the 5-pt turns out to be the second best play in this position.

They point is that Red is still 15 pips behind after the roll although that is not a true count. He needs to block White as well as he can and the play to do that is 8/5, 6/2.

I don’t think many players would find this move over the board (my opponent didn’t) and would do something with the outside checkers. Once you understand the logic you can see the play but the vast majority would miss this in live play.
