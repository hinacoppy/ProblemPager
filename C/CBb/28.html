pips Red=210 White=70

Red certainly shouldn’t play 22/20 with the 2. This gives White pick-and-pass and pointing numbers at a time when Red wants to build a prime to contain a hit checker.

The question is where to build that prime? Normally you would build your home board but here Red’s timing precludes that option. He should use the two points he already has (8-pt and 10-pt) and build around those.

13/10, 11/9 starts a new point and makes a third point in the prime and is easily the best play. Once you understand the logic the best play is clear but it is getting to that logic in an unfamiliar position that is the difficult bit.
