Money Game. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=79 White=61
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
It is always gratifying when a ‘known' book position occurs in real life. As Tim Chow has commented this is in Trice's “Boot Camp” and I have seen it elsewhere.

I know that this is a redouble and a take because it is in my backgammon model. You can work this out from first principles to establish that White wins more than 25% of the time (in fact it is nearly 29%) but it is easier if you just keep this as one of your reference positions.

Given that the cube has enormous value to White in this position he can actually take with less than 25% winning chances. If White has a five-point home board then Red does not have a redouble.

This occurred in the Battersea chouette. Red redoubled – White dropped in a flash. It really is worth learning those reference positions!
