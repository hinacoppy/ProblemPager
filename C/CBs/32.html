Money play. How should Red play 62?
pips Red=128 White=99
Red wants to do three thigs: cover the blot on his ace-point; escape a rear checker (with his three rear checkers on one point he could get into difficulty with something like 55) and provide outer board coverage in case White escapes.

Take one of the spare checkers on White’s 5-pt and put it on his 3-pt, giving him a four-point home board, and Red must play 9/1.

However, the blot on White’s 3-pt gives Red tactical possibilities. He can fulfil two of his objectives with 21/13 and that is the correct play by a long way. Now 9/1 is nearly a double blunder.
