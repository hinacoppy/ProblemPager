Match Play. Red trails 6-11 to 13. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=131 White=133
For money this is a clear no double/take – Red has too much work to do.

At this score, however, the gammons that Red will win are very powerful and so he should double. To not turn the cube here is a double blunder.

Despite the gammon threat White has a clear take and two points win the match for him exactly. Passing would be a blunder.

The position demonstrates the very big impact that a skewed match score can have on ‘normal’ cube decisions.
