Money Play. How should Red play 22?
pips Red=111 White=131
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Play without much though and you will choose 20/18(2), 8/4 or 20/18(2), 6/4(2).

If you look deeper you will see that these plays leave the rear two checkers somewhat stranded and you may be forced off White's bar-point just when you don't want to be.

Then you will find 20/16(2)! The blot on Red's 4-pt isn't in that much danger and 20/16(2) puts pressure on the blot on Red's 10-pt. When ahead in the race, race!

Because I set this as a problem you may have found 20/16(2). Over the board the play would be missed over 90% of the time.
