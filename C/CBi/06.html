Back to theme of broken primes!

I’m sure nearly everybody would hit here (I did) with either 13/4* or 10/5, 8/4*, there is little to choose between those two plays.

However, Red is running out of time to free his back checker and he could end up closing out two White checkers but still be unable to free his own rear checker.

Although it is close the correct play is 24/20/15. This triplicates White’s 3’s and if Red is not hit (there are only 10 shots) then he is in a very strong position. Even if he is hit there will be multiple return shots. Before the bots nobody would have thought of playing 24/15 but the game has evolved hugely in the last 20 years.
