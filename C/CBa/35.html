Match Play. Double Match Point. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=34 White=28
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one was included for a bit of fun. A few rolls earlier Red had a checker on White’s 2-pt and White had three checkers on his 3-pt and two on his 1-pt. At this point Red’s winning chances were under 1%. White rolled 32 and then Red hit the blot to reach this position a couple of rolls later.

There is only 0.014 equity difference between the two plays but 6/off, 4/off wins by a whisker as it means Red remains with an even number of checkers to bear off.

Needless to say Red won the match after White fanned a few times. You can assume White was not amused!!
