He looked at bar/22, 6/4* (a quadruple blunder) and bar/23, 13/11 (a septuple blunder) for quite some time but the position cries out for the hit with bar/23, 18/15*.

The three blots might look scary but the play puts a second White checker behind a four-point prime and stops White's attack. Red trails by 49 pips before the roll and so the hit is clearly indicated.

White responded with an innocuous 51 and Red soon won the game by building a prime.
