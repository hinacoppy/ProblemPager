The key here is not just to focus on making the bar-point. Those three rear checkers need to start on their journey home. 15/11, 9/7 is therefore the weakest of the possible plays.

The best play is 23/21, 15/11, providing double coverage of the bar-point in case of the dreaded 61 by White, and keeping the anchor for the time being in case of disaster. 23/17 and 21/15 are both OK and even 21/17, 15/13 is not an error.
