pips Red=158 White=175

The first inclination is to hit with the 6 and then look for a 2. However, any hitting play is an error and some of them are blunders.

Red is ahead in the race and outboarded so logic says he should race. White has only seven checkers in the attack zone. All of this points to a running play. 22/14 is reasonable but slightly better is 22/16, 13/11. This keeps the rear checkers in communication and there is meaningful triplication of fours.

Not an immediately obvious play but once you see 22/16, 13/11 the more you appreciate it.
