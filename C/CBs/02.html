Money Play. How should Red Play 11?
pips Red=168 White=166
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is a complex position and probably worthy of a longer article. Red has many thing he can do and wants to do but the key thing he needs to understand is that he needs to play this as a prime-versus-prime position. His best way to win and escape those three rear checkers is to crack White’s five-point prime.

The best move to achieve this 23/22, 12/11*, 8/7, 6/5*. If things go well Red will have five White checkers behind a four- or five-point prime and his forward position may collapse. The second-best move is 12/11*, 8/7(2), 6/5 with the same idea. It swaps potentially freeing a back checker with a 6 for the solid asset of the bar-point. This turns out to be an error but I wouldn’t criticise anyone for making the play.

8/5*, 6/5* is a double blunder but one I would expect a lot of players to make over the board. However, Red will need both his bar-point and his 5-pt and so 23/22, 12/11*, 8/7, 6/5* is the correct play.
