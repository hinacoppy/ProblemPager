Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=81 White=134
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Basic backgammon theory says that you should wait to double a back game until you have only three points to clear in front of the defender's front-most anchor.

That is the case here and so Red should certainly double.

Unlike most back games Red has room to park checkers behind White's anchors and so when I saw this position I assumed White must have a clear drop,

This turns out not to be the case and this position is right on the borderline and White can take or pass. One reason is that when White does lose he loses fewer gammons than when his anchors are further back in Red's home board. Thus, he needs to win a lower percentage than normal to be able to accept the double.

Interestingly if you take a checker from White's 2-pt and put it on his mid-point the take becomes trivial because White won't have to give up one of his back game anchors so quickly.
