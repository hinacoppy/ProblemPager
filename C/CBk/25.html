The problem with 8/5 for the 3 is that the 6 must be played 13/7, leaving four blots one of which is behind a broken prime. That is just too much risk, coupled with fact that Red’s rear checker will still be behind that broken prime.

The correct play is 23/14 which escapes that checker and there is some mild duplication of White’s 2s. Even after this best play Red is only a 51-49 favourite because of the disjointed nature of his position.
