As you would expect there isn't much equity between the various plays because Red's position is so poor whatever he does. However, in such positions one shouldn't just up but always try to maximise one's equity.

Red's best chance is to steer for a 1-2 back game and so after bar/23* he should choose between, 11/6, 8/3, and 6/1. ^/1 puts a checker in the wrong place and so the choice lies between the other two and on a rollout there is no difference between them.

Of course the position is here because of what happened. The team played, bar/23*/18. White fanned with 66 and then Red rolled 41, playing 18/14*, 6/5. You can guess the rest and of course Red got in a well-timed redouble as well.
