Money Play. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=141 White=92
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red can't hold everything - 7/3, 6/1 is not sensible.

Ideally Red would like to keep his 16-pt as it holds it both White's mid-point and 10-pt. This would imply either 22/13 or 20/11.

Trailing in the race 22/13 can't be right and so 20/11 must be considered. The alternative is to hold the rear position and play 16/7 which makes the bar-point and holds the 20-pt and the blot on the 22-pt.

As you might expect 22/13 is virtually a blunder. 20/11 is just marginally (0.018) better than 16/7. The key here is to consider 20/11 at all. Many would quickly reject it because it gives up the golden point but each position has its own particular demands and here Red wants to hold the 22-pt blot and the 16-pt anchor and so 20/11 is the correct play.
