16/8 is a double blunder, giving White a great 53 and Red a horrible 33. 23/15 gives Red more cover numbers next time but crucially no direct cover of Red's bar-pt and 8-pt should White enter and escape. However the two moves are virtually identical.

The correct move is 23/18, 16/13 giving good outfield coverage and as noted above, coverage of the 8-pt and bar-pt. By the way if you chose 16/11, 6/3 go to the bottom of the class as that takes away White's horror numbers, 31 and 32.

After any of the sensible moves, it is double/pass if White fans. White just doesn't get the checker home often enough and the constant threat of entering with 31 or 32 is always there as well.
