Money Game. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=110 White=128
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
When I had this position I very quickly played 6/4, 5/2 and gave the move no real thought. It seemed logical to slot the next required point and then the 2 plays itself.

Analysing the game with XG it flagged the move as an error and in a rollout it didn't change its opinion. The difference was that Red lost more gammons after the slotting play than 8/6, 8/5 which is XG's play, presumably in variations where the blot on the 2-pt is picked up.

The other advantage to 8/6, 8/5 that I can see is that after White hits the Red blot on his 4-pt, Red's entering numbers such as 16, 26, 15 and 25 play better.

Normally I am in accord with XG. On this one I need to do some more work before I am completely convinced but the fact that I need to do more analysis turns what seemed like a mundane decision into a learning opportunity. Thus is progress made.
