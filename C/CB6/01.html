Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=97 White=70
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Should Red play the trap play 8/5, 8/5 so that White has to play his sixes? Experience tells us that such plays should be executed when your opponent has only a two-point home board.

That would imply it is too risky here and that is correct. Red needs to position his checkers to catch any fleeing White checker and he would also like to make his 4-pt. It is a choice between 17/10, and 17/13, 14/11. These plays have identical equity and are both better than 14/7 which reduces outer board coverage.
