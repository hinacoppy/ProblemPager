Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=108 White=173
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This position is an object lesson in moving checkers round the board while your opponent is on the bar. Historically people played these positions far too safely.

The key point is that the blots protect themselves insomuch as if White does enter from the bar and hit, he may have to give up a point to do so, exposing two blots, or he may have to hit loose even though Red still has a five-point board.

Over the board 21/13 was played but that is too safe and leaves Red susceptible to rolling a bad double later on.

The correct play is 18/15, 18/13 taking the risk now while White is still on the bar. It looks very risky but any other play is at least a bad error.
