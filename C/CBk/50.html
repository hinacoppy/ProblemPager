Money Play. How should Red play 21?
pips Red=142 White=120
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red can play safe with 8/5, take a small risk with 11/10, 11/9 or play big with 11/9, 8/7. Which shall it be?

8/5 is clearly wrong – it does nothing to improve Red’ s position. The key is the doubling cube. If Red slots the bar and White rolls neither a 5 or a 1 he will have to pass Red’s double. If he does creep up with a 1 Red will still have a powerful, but takeable cube.

11/10, 11/9 is a reasonable play but still a small error.
