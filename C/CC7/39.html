However, the back game player must be able to contain a hit checker, and so his home board needs to be nigh on perfect. In this position White has a gap on his 4-pt and although he may make that point there is no guarantee that he will.

That turns a close take into a close pass. This is a useful reference position and reinforces the need to make your home board points in order when playing a back game.
