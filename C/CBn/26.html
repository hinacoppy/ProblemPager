Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=155 White=117
It will surprise many that 8/3, 6/3 and 24/16 are virtually equal in equity with the pure play of making the 3-pt coming out fractionally ahead!

This is the second position in the last few weeks where purity has outweighed a tactical hit. The hit gives white some chances to play against Red's blots as Red no longer has an anchor and consequently Red will lose quite a few gammons. If Red makes the pure play he should not have too many problems escaping White's home board.

I admit that when I saw this position my instinct was to hit. This is the sort of position you should manually play it out a few times to get comfortable with the recommended play but XG is rarely wrong.
