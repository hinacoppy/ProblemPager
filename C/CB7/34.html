Money Play. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=19 White=30
This is a question of what formulae you use for bear-offs and how much time and effort you put into learning them.

This is a rolls vs pips position on which the late Walter trice did a lot of work.

Red has a virtual no miss five roll position so his effective pip count will be close to 7n+1 where n is the number of rolls. This gives him an EPC of 36 plus a bit for the fact that he may roll three ones. Let’s say his EPC his 36.5

White’s EPC is his pip cunt (30) plus his wastage. The flat position (two checkers on each point) wastes 10 pips. White is better than that here so over the board I would estimate a wastage of 8 pips.

Now read the “Backgammon Races” article in the downloads section of this website and you will find Trice’s Rules for evaluating such positions. You will then find that this a redouble and a take.
