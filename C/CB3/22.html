Money Play. How should Red play 21?
pips Red=160 White=211
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The two key points on the board are Red's 5-pt and his bar-pt. All his efforts should be going into making those points. With four checkers back he is unlikely to make them naturally so the quickest way to make them is to slot them and then cover them.

Obviously it is far less risky to slot the bar-pt but equally well the reward is much greater if the 5-pt can be made. Over the board I played 15/13, 8/7 which turns out to be the second-best play. XG believes that slotting the 5-pt with 15/13, 6/5 is correct, but only by 0.010 after a long rollout. All other moves are at least errors.

The keys here are a) to have a very clear plan as to how to play the position and the objectives you are trying to achieve b) make sure you consider all plays even if you reject them for seemingly valid reasons.
