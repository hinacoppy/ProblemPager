2015 - Position 92

Money Game. How should Red play 44?
pips Red=152 White=157
When I had this position I fairly quickly played 13/9*/5, 7/3(2)*. My opponent entered with one checker and I cashed the game.

I was amazed when I put this into XG that it preferred 8/4(2), 7/3(2)*. How can hitting one possibly be better than hitting two? All three of the ‘hit two’ plays have the same equity but they are 0.05 behind the hit one play.

Normally when XG recommends a move I can quickly see why but here it took some time. What I think is going on is this:

After the hit one play the only roll that allows White to take Red’s double next turn is 26 (bar/23/17*) and that is no bargain. After the hit two plays White can take after 11, 22 and 44 but now he has a much better position.

It would seem that this possibility of White anchoring with an effective double is the difference between the plays which I think you would agree is something you wouldn’t have thought about when playing Red’s 44 – I certainly didn’t!
