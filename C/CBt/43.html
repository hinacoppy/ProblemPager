The prime must be broken, the question is how to do that?

If we combine the concept of putting the checkers where they want to go with giving White as many bad rolls as possible then it is not hard to find 7/2, 5/2. Now with 31 and 32 White has to break his anchor without fully escaping even one checker.

The play nicely diversifies Red’s number – he enters with big numbers and hits with small ones.
