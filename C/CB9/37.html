Money Play. How should Red play 31?
pips Red=52 White=40
4/off is the play made on automatic. However, the blot on White’s 4-pt creates tactical opportunities and if White enters next roll Red would rather be hit than not.

That implies 3/off for the 3 – what about the 1? 6/5 is an option but the best play, perhaps surprisingly is 5/4. This puts another checker on the 4-pt (always useful) but also gives Red a double shot if White enters with a double 5. This is something that is not obvious on first analysis.

This is actually quite a difficult problem and highly instructive. Any move other than 5/4, 3/off is an error.

As I have said before backgammon is a very difficult game!
