Interestingly both those who commented on this position looked at or chose 24/22, 13/9 which is the move I chose over the board. This turns out to be nearly a blunder.

Virtually any splitting play is a blunder. White has 10 checkers in the attack zone and a better home board than Red whose frontward position has some weaknesses.

Red does better to try to build a strong home board and/or a prime on his side of the board and leave those back checkers well alone.

Most of the time when I make errors/blunders they are easy to understand in retrospect but it took me some time before I agreed with my silicon friend, XG, on this one.

As a result I felt I had learned something very useful from studying this position.
