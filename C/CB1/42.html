Money Game. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=103 White=72
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
When I was a small boy I was taught that you didn't execute trap plays until the opponent had only a two-point home board. I was therefore surprised when I checked this position with XG and saw it's strong recommendation for 8/5, 8/3.

As ever I rolled it out a few thousand times and that changed the picture. Now the trap play comes out equal with 19/16, 6/1. The optimal placement of Red's outfield checkers and the blot on White's 4-pt makes the trap play viable but any variation and the trap play becomes too dangerous.

So my lesson all those years ago was accurate.
