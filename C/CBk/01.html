Money Play. How should Red play 22?
pips Red=127 White=177
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
While Red still has a rear checker to escape Red should create his own four-point prime and so 11/9(2) is very clear.

After that Red should not play 12/8, stacking five checkers on his 8-pt and creating potential problems with 3’s and 4’s. There are two sensible choices:

8/6(2) creating bear-in flexibility or 3/1(2) taking the ace-point away from White as a possible re-entry point and hopefully creating room for Red to dump his checkers as he bears in. Very few people would spot this option.

The two plays dead-heat on the rollout so take your choice!
