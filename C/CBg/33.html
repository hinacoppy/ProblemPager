Money Play. How should Red play 63?
pips Red=132 White=114
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Unless you have seen this sort of position before you are unlikely to get it right. I first came across a similar position in an Othello Quiz about five years ago.

This is essentially a prime versus prime position and so Red needs to put his checkers to work to build a prime or broken prime. A play such as 10/1 is a do nothing play that doesn’t improve Red’s position at all, in fact it weakens it.

The correct play and the only one that isn’t a blunder, is the double slot 10/4, 6/3!! This puts the checkers where Red wants them and gives him good winning chances even occasionally after being hit.

Given this as a quiz problem a reasonable percentage of players will get this right. Over the board I suspect that would drop to no more than 10%.
