These final two problems are variants of positions from Mary Hickey and Marty Storer's upcoming book, which they let me have a sneak peek at.

At gammon save, losing a gammon is twice as costly as usual, and gammon wins have no extra value. Safe play is usually indicated.
Here, it may seem automatic to safety the outfield blot with 14/13 and then play either 5/3 or 3/1.
The trouble is that we still have that 22pt blot to worry about.
White will gladly hit loose and initiate a blot-hitting contest, when our weak home board will become a serious liability.
Priority number one is to strengthen our board with 3/2, and then 14/12 falls out as the rest of the play.
Note that if it were DMP then 13/10* would probably be best, but it loses too many gammons at this score.
