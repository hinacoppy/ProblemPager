Money Game. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=84 White=54
So did you keep White's ace-point? If you did you were in error.

If you were fearful of losing a gammon by being pointed on after playing 24/20, 4/3 then you were guilty of mis-analysis. Red loses more gammons by hanging back when he can't free either checker for another couple of rolls than being pointed on.

Breaking the home board with something like 5/1, 5/4 also reduces Red's winning chances after he eventually hits a shot. He must maintain his five-point home board. He keeps alive his chances of winning after a hit and also in the race (admittedly slim).

And of course on a good day White will roll 65!
