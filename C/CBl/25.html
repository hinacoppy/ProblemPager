Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=7 White=16
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is a ‘Rolls versus Pips” position best handled with Trice’s formulae. For a full understanding of these read my monograph on racing formulae in the Downloads section of this website.

In brief Red’s EPC (Effective Pip Count) is 22. White’s actual Pip Count is 16. Then you need to add his wastage which I would estimate to be between 6 and pips (note how bad White’s fours are for him).

To have a take in a three-roll position White’s EPC must be no greater than Red’s. Six pips of wastage give him a borderline take but 7 pips make it a drop. XG gives White’s EPC as 22.4 thus the position is double and drop. Moving White’s checker on his 2-pt to his 1-pt makes the position the skinniest of takes.
