Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=168 White=126
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is an exercise in logic but a lot of people, including this writer, would get it wrong over the board. Red wants to prime or attack White’s lone back checker. At the same time, he is 35 pips behind in the race after the roll so ideally he wants to hold the 23-pt rather than the 20-pt especially as for the moment White’s board is no threat. 23/20, 13/9 should be considered but ultimately rejected.

The blot on White’s ace-point gives Red tactical possibilities so 20/13 is far too weak a play. He could play 20/16, 8/5 but that takes a builder away from the bar-point. The best move is 20/16, 13/10, playing to the game plan outlined above.

Any other move is at least an error.
