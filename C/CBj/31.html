Match Play. Red trails 3-6 to 11. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=128 White=125
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I had this somewhat unusual position against Wayne Felton at the Cotswold tournament last weekend. After long thought I doubled and after even longer thought Wayne passed. How did we do?

White’s position looks parlous as his board is about to crash and he needs a 1 and three 6’s to escape Red’s board. However, if Red fans and White rolls a 6 or a 4 his position improves significantly. For money, because of White’s recube potential, this a very clear no double and an easy take.

At the score Red should be quicker to double because White’s redoubles are not nearly so powerful as winning a gammon with the cube on 4 gives him more points than he needs (overage). Even so, this is a borderline double and a trivial take. Wayne overestimated Red’s gammon chances and kindly gave me a point.
