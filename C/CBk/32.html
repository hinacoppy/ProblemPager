Money Play. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=167 White=120
Obviously Red will make his bar and that is best done by 8/7. The real question is how to play the 6?

The two contenders are 13/7 and 21/15. 21/15 is right for a number of reasons:

• It is not clear Red wants the spare from the mid-point on the bar-point. It may better placed helping to make an outside point.

• 21/15 acts as a deflection play. White will have to give up a point to hit it and he really won't want to give up his mid-point.

• Red doesn't want to be pointed on or hit loose on White's 4-pt, giving White the chance to make that key point.

• If not hit it acts as builder for the 9-pt and 10-pt.
