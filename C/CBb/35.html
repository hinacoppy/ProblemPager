Money Play. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=127 White=146

Red must advance to the edge of the prime with 24/22. 8/4, 6/4 is the wrong idea, needlessly exposing a blot and trying to prime a single checker is an incorrect game plan.

The question is whether to play 13/9 or the odd looking 6/2?

13/9 uses the last spare from the mid-point and exposes a blot to five shots at a time when White has the better board. Odd though it looks 6/2 is the right play and 24/22, 13/9 is a blunder. For once the ugly play wins the day.
