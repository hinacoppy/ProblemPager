Money Play. How should Red play 54? If the cube is in the centre does that alter the play?
pips Red=91 White=145
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
A very useful position which reminded me of a key learning point.

Firstly, with a centred cube, Red should double and White should pass – it is a double blunder to take. If Red doesn't double then he should not give White a free shot to win the game by playing 13/9, 13/8. That play is a double blunder. He should play 8/3, 5/1 and re-evaluate next turn and hopefully do better!

With the cube on White's side of the board it is a different matter and Red must use the checkers to win. After 8/3, 5/1 Red's structure is poor and he will need a lot of luck to bring the position home without leaving any shots. So much so that he should pay now with 13/9, 13/8.

There is quite a bit of life after death even if he is hit – one of the key considerations in ‘pay me now, pay me later' problems.

It is a small error to play safe with 8/3, 5/1 with White holding the cube.
