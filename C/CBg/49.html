Match Play. Red leads 4-2 to 5 (post-Crawford). How should Red play 62?
pips Red=167 White=159
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red will be doubled after his roll and must take.

To limit the gammon risk he should keep his ace-point anchor and play with one blot by moving 13/5. This is one of the few scenarios where 13/5 is correct instead of the normal 24/18, 13/11 which is correct for money and at most other match scores. If this were the Crawford game 24/18, 13/11 would be the correct play.

At this score 24/18, 13/11 is an error.
