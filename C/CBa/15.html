Match Play. Red trails 4-5 to 7. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=119 White=135
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Another position to graphically demonstrate the difference between match play and money play.

For money a double here would be grossly premature and a double blunder. At thIs match score not doubling would be a double blunder! Gammons are meaningless to White and the cube is dead after Red doubles. The position is volatile and a gammon wins the match for Red. All of this adds up to a very clear double. It is a trivial take for White.

Always remember Bill Robertie’s advice: “In a match always know what the score is and at the start of each game, before you even roll one dice, make sure you know what the correct strategy is for your particular score.” That piece of advice has won me a lot of matches over the years.
