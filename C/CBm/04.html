(CBm-04)   2017 - Position 126
Match Play. Red trails 4-8 to 11. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=105 White=23
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is all about the efficiency of numbers and crossovers.

Re should take two crossovers with 10/5, 8/5 or 10/5, 9/6 – the two plays have identical equities.

The key is to avoid 13/8, 9/6 which looks efficient but isn’t. Red won’t get proper value from his subsequent fives and sixes his one will also play purely. His best future 1 is likely to be 13/12 and then he can use a six efficiently.

It’s remarkable just how bad 13/8, 9/6 is as a play. It is a significant blunder. Saving the gammon is not the most exciting part of backgammon but it is important to understand the principles involved and to learn how to use (and plan to use) numbers efficiently.
