Match Play. Red leads 1-0 to 5. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=143 White=161
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
For money, this would be a double and a very clear take. In fact, it is only just a double. White’s four-point prime means that Red will have to tread warily and may not be able to go for an all–out blitz.

At the match score the double is much clearer as a doubled gammon gives Red the match with perfect efficiency. Now not doubling is a 1.5 blunder, demonstrating just how much you have to think about the cube in short matches.
