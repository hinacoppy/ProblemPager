Money Play. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=115 White=51
Switching points – the play made at the table – doesn't figure in XG's top 15 plays!

Red needs to hit loose and keep his broken prime so 5/3* is two of the 1's. There is little to choose between 8/7(2), 5/3* and 14/12, 5/3*. The former wins out by a short head because then the checker on the 14-pt is six pips away from the open 8-pt and optimises the use of Red's checkers.

The key here is in understanding how to play the position. The prime is easier to build after hitting loose and it also has the tactical benefits that 32 and 33 compromise White's home board. Switching points is a double or triple blunder depending upon how the other two 1's are played.
