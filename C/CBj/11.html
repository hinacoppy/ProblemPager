After 8/3, 5/1 White can play most rolls safely and Red will be hoping for a miracle double on the very next roll. After 17/8, even if White hits, a lot of the time Red will have one roll to roll the crucial 2 and stay in the game.

Interestingly after 17/8, Red wins 1% less games but wins 4.5% more gammons because of the hits on the 2-pt. 8/3, 5/1 loses 6% more gammons because after that play White can still have two blots picked up.

Overall 8/3, 5/1 gives up 0.05 points of equity so an error but not a blunder.
