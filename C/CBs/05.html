Money Play. How should Red play 51?
pips Red=88 White=54
You should never break your home board unless you have to, and this position is no exception. 6/1, 5/1 is a bad error. If you give up the 6-pt you will have trouble restraining a hit checker.

The correct play is 19/14, 2/1 which keeps the rear checkers connected and slots the ace-point. Yes, Red will be hit quite often but the play generates multiple return shots and, in the cases where White rolls a big number and safeties one checker (for example, 65 is played 15/4), Red gets a direct shot at the other one.
