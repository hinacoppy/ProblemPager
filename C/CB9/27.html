Match Play. Red leads 4-1 to 7. How should Red play 66?
pips Red=141 White=149
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Although 9/3(2) looks neat the right play by a long way is 24/18(2), 13/(7), locking in the White checkers on Red’s ace-point.

For money 24/18(2), 9/3(2) would be a blunder but at this match score it is a double blunder because in a lot of variations Red will want to play on for an undoubled gammon to get him to 6-1 (Crawford). That extra point is extremely valuable. On a minor point 24/18(2), 13/7(2) duplicates White’s hitting aces.
