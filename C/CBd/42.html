Money Play. How should Red play 22?
pips Red=127 White=114
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Should Red give up his prime to switch points? The answer to that is no – he still has too much work to do. He does better to maintain his prime. He would like to hit the White blot on the 2-pt but again he shouldn’t give up his 8-pt to do.

Equally well he shouldn’t race when behind so the weak 20/14, 15/13 is definitely not correct and that blot on White’s 5-pt should remain there to hassle White’s checkers on that side of the board.

The best play is 14/8, 15/13, hoping that White doesn’t roll a 5 so Red can attack the blot on the 2-pt with a 6 next turn. 14/6 is the second best play but it is still nearly an error.

This was a difficult position with lots of factors influencing the decision-making – I suspect only a very low percentage of readers would have found the best play here. Such is the complexity of backgammon.
