Match Play. Red leads 2-1 to 5. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=79 White=53
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This position demonstrates the power of getting to Crawford with your opponent on an odd score.

For money this would be ludicrous double but the match play equities make this a very clear double for Red. If he misses most of the time he will be dropping White's redouble but the advantage of getting to 4-1 when he does hit adequately compensates for his losses.

In fact not doubling is a blunder (just). This position acts as a reminder that you must keep the match score in your mind all the time. It is far too easy to play on automatic in such positions and make a decision based on money play.
