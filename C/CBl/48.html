Money Play. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=156 White=148
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I think most people would miss the best play here unless given it as a quiz problem.

Over the board I played 22/16, 22/18 without a huge amount of thought. It was only in post-match analysis that I found 22/16, 20/16. Giving up two anchors might seem bold but it is logical.

The 16-pt provides a stepping stone for Red to escape his rear checkers at a time when White only has nine checkers in the attack zone. White will be loth to have one or more checkers sent back behind Red’s very useful four-point prime. 22/16, 22/18 turns out to be a blunder.

If Red had rolled 52 it would have been a toss-up between 22/15, releasing the rear anchor, and 8/3, 5/3, taking the solid asset. In any position, having solved the initial problem I always like to test out a few more moves to get a good understanding of the position and the appropriate game plans – a technique that has served me well over the years.
