Money Play. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=127 White=129
What is fascinating here is just how bad are all the moves other the correct one!

10/7, 10/6 is a bad blunder as is 13/6. 13/10, 13/9 is only a bad error!

The correct play is 13/10, 6/2. This continues to block White’s sixes from Red’s 4-pt and crucially, makes the extra home board point at a time when a hitting contest may ensue.

Assuming White doesn’t hit the blot on the mid-point Red will have two useful spares to play with next turn.

Once you think it through the play is clear but it is too easy over the board to play 10/7, 10/6, minimising shots.
