This is a close call. If White splits the back checkers to take advantage of the blot on White’s 5-pt and to get more shots on most of White’s sixes, then he wins more games but loses more gammons. The trade-off is very close and none of the sensible moves can be classed as errors.

The rollout has 19/17, 8/5 fractionally ahead of 8/5, 6/4 but by the slimmest of margins. The key is to understand that splitting the rearmost checkers is an option.
