This turns out to be more difficult than I thought.

Do you play bar/24, 11/6 after which the position is not volatile enough for White to double or alternatively, do you play bar/20, 8/7*, after which White has a clear double due to the higher volatility?

From an equity perspective the two plays are identical. As you would expect, bar/20, 8/7* winds more games but loses more gammons. Your decision should probably be made by evaluating your opponent’s strengths and skills. Of course, if he won’t double after bar/20, 8/7* then that is clearly the right play. Similarly, if he would double after bar/24, 11/6 then that is the right play! Whoever said backgammon was an easy game?
