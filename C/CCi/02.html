Match Play. Red trails 1-5 to to 11. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=143 White=154
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
For money this is a borderline double and a trivial take. With only nine checkers in the zone and only one cover number for the ace-point blot Red could be forgiven for waiting.
At this score, however, the double becomes mandatory because of the gammon threat (35% chance). White’s take is still very easy because of the strength of his home board and no weaknesses other than his two checkers on the bar.
If you move a checker from Red’s 6-pt to his 5-pt, giving him two cover numbers for the ace-point blot, then the position becomes double and pass. To quote Robertie once again, “everything matters”.
