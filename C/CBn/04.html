Money Play. How should Red play 65?
pips Red=111 White=127
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
I really like this one!

My opponent played 8/2, 6/1 which is definitely not the right idea. I expected 16/11, 8/2, duplicating 4’s, to be the correct play but was astonished when XG flagged this play as a blunder as well. I still couldn’t ‘see’ the right play.

That play is 16/11, 16/10 leaving a voluntary triple shot!! What a play!

Once you see it the logic is inescapable. The play duplicates White’s 1’s and 3’s and to hit White will probably have to create blots of his own at a time when Red has the stronger home board.

If White elects not to hit or can’t hit then Red will have cleared a major hurdle by clearing his 16-pt. The play takes the adage of ‘when ahead in the race, race’ to a new extreme.

Note that if White already had a four-point home board then 8/2, 6/1 would be the right play by a long way. Backgammon is truly a game of small differences.
