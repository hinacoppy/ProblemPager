Money Play. How should Red play 63?
pips Red=142 White=143
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Over time you learn not to make plays like bar/22, 9/3. It looks ugly and it is.

Red must choose between the relatively passive bar/22/16 and the aggressive bar/22, 8/2*. The latter tries to take advantage of White's blots while Red has the stronger board. It also follows the general strategy for such positions of attacking a lone rear checker.

Bar/22, 8/2* is the correct play. Bar/22/16 is not quite an error but once more the award goes to the aggressive play.
