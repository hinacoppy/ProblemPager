Money Play. a) How should red play 32?
pips Red=120 White=113
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
b) If White owns the cube how should Red play 32?
c) If the cube is centred how should Play 32?


Position submitted by Chris Purchase

This is similar in theme to the problem I posted over Christmas in that the correct play changes according to the cube position.

With an owned cube (i.e. Red has access to the cube) Red can play cautiously with 13/11, 13/10 (don’t even think about breaking that excellent five-point home board). A hit is not disastrous and on White’s small numbers Red will be posed to attack next turn.

With a centred cube gammons are not activated and so the logical play is 13/10, 6/4*, seeking to quickly reach a strong doubling position and taking advantage of the strong home board. Note that after 13/10, 6/4* and a fan by White, Red is not quite strong enough to double.

If the opponent owns the cube then Red must win with the checkers alone and therefore needs to be more aggressive and try to finish the game as soon as possible. The correct play is therefore 13/10, 6/4*. His anchor protects Red from any complete disasters and if White fans then Red’s gammon prospects are excellent.

It is important to understand the underlying point here that once you have given the cube away and can only win using the checkers then you must use those checkers as effectively and efficiently as possible.
