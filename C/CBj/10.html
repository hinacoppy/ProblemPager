Money Play. How should Red play 66?
pips Red=146 White=175
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
22/16, 13/7(3) looks obvious and is the play I made over the board. It triplicates White’s fours so what more could you want?

The bots put a high premium on escaping checkers and XG pointed me in the direction of 22/16, 21/5, 13/7(2) leaving three blots! Once you see it the play it becomes clear. At a stroke it escapes Red’s rear checkers and it duplicates White’s 1’s, 2’s and 4’s. Any 5 or 6 by White next turn will lead to a cube that White will nearly certainly have to pass.

This is a lesson in logic but not one that many would spot unaided. It is one of the reasons that playing strength has improved so much in the last twenty years. A good teacher can really help you in anything you do and XG really is a very good teacher indeed!

I learnt a lot from this position and it is a technique that would do well to remember.
