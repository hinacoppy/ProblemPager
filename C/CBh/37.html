Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=133 White=159
An unusual position and difficult to judge: a 4 by Red and a fan by White is a definite market losing sequence but there is a myriad of variations.

I would apply Woolseyâ€™s Law and double and thus give the problem to White. When things go well Red will win an easy gammon. From his perspective White has to hope his broken five-point prime will do the trick. In fact it does it often enough, coupled with the possibility of a 2-5 back game, to make it a trivial take.

The volatility is enough to warrant an initial double. However if Red already owns the cube then he should wait as gammons are already activated.
