Match Play – Double Match Point. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=118 White=199
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The correct play is the same at DMP as for money – essentially because the approach doesn't change.

If White enters on Red's 2-pt Red will hit to make sure White doesn't get a well-timed back game. So Red needs to bring builders in range of the 2-pt. He also wants to think about dismantling his mid-point as looking ahead that will be the most difficult point to clear. The checker on White's 5-pt is in no danger and can be cleared later.

The obvious plays are 13/9, 8/6 and 13/11, 13/9. There is no real need to give White a great double four and the 8-pt may yet prove useful. Therefore the correct play is 13/11, 13/9. All other plays are at least errors.

I was White in this position. My opponent woodenly played 20/14 and later lost the game when he couldn't clear his mid-point safely.
