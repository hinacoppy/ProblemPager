Money Play. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=137 White=144
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Another one where perhaps the obvious play is wrong.

8/3, 7/3 looks good but leaves Red with an inflexible structure. 13/8, 7/3 playing safe is also the wrong idea. Red has the better board and should use the asset.

Many would play 24/20, 13/8 but that leaves too many weaknesses. The correct play is 13/4! With many of his 2's and 3's White won't be hitting. The move gives Red builders for the 3-pt and also lets him make his bar-pt or 8-pt next time. The added strength of Red's home board means that White must play cautiously as a hit could prove fatal.

I think that 24/20, 13/8 would be the popular choice but surprisingly that is a 0.062 error. As I have often remarked, backgammon really is a very difficult game.
