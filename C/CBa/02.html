Money Play. How should Red play 51?
pips Red=150 White=160
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one is tricky. Over the board Red tried 24/23, 9/4 which is a long way wrong. He doesn’t want to anchor on White’s 2-pt.

Any of the hitting plays are far too open and so really the choice comes down to strengthening the home board with 10/5, 6/5 or advancing 23/22 (preparing to escape or anchor on the 22-pt) combined with safetying the blot on the 9-pt by moving 9/4.

The former wins more games and gammons but also loses more gammons. The rollout gives the nod to the pure 10/5, 6/5 but it is close and in a chouette I wouldn’t argue with a captain who wanted to play 23/22, 9/4.

10/5, 6/5 looks bold but never underestimate the long-term benefits of that 5-pt asset.
