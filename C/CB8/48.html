Money Play. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=104 White=123
This is a sort of pay me now, pay me later problem. If Red plays 6/3, 4/2 he could have trouble with numbers like 42 and 33 next time and he leaves a very inflexible position.

If he plays 18/13, then any 1 will give Red plenty of return shots and even 66 from White leaves 12 shots. 18/13 carries some risks but backgammon is a game of risk and reward and here the gains outweigh the risks.

6/3, 4/2 is worse than a blunder and the half-way house play of 18/15, 4/2 is very nearly a blunder. 18/16, 6/3 and 18/15, 6/4 are both virtually double blunders.

Maximum flexibility and minimum voluntary shots are gained by 18/13 so that is the correct play by a long way.
