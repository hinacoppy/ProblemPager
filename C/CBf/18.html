Red doesn’t have the ammunition to attack and close out White and so 24/22, 6/1* is much too ambitious.

The choice lies between the very tidy looking 20/13 and releasing a back checker into a triple shot with 24/17.

Whereas thing can go badly wrong after 20/13 that is not the case after 24/17, precisely because Red still holds the golden-point anchor. Notice that if White does hit he has to give up one of his stripped points (unless he rolls a double).

20/13 looks neat but it is the wrong game plan entirely and is actually a blunder. It is rare that giving up the golden-point is correct and this certainly isn’t an instance.
