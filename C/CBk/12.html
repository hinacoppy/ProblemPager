Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=103 White=46
I had this position in a GridGammon match. I decided I didn't want to kill another checker and so played 23/18, 7/4. The problem with this is that the checker on the 23-pt really wants to stay there but advancing any of the other checkers brings them too close to White's checker.

18/13, 15/13 has merit as it leaves some root numbers for White (61,52,43) but does leave a direct shot and doesn't keep the rear checkers spread.

I went on to win the game by constantly hitting White's checker and building a 5-pt home board but post match analysis shows that the best move is 19/16, 7/2 actually killing another checker. The play blocks 55, makes 64 a root number, maintains the checker on the 23-pt and doesn't leave a direct shot - a reasonable return for the dead checker.

The priority here is hitting the checker, not building a board sometime in the future.
