Money Play. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=141 White=200
Red does not want to give White the opportunity to make a third deep anchor and potentially play a 2-4 or 3-4 back game.

Once you understand the game plan then the best play is easy to find: 8/3*, 6/2*. White’s home board position is no real threat so Red can happily make this bold play. If his checkers are hit he will have no trouble recirculating them and if not hit he has hopefully forced White into a 4-5 back game.

There is still a lot of play in the position but 8/3*, 6/2* gives Red his best chance of winning the game. Remember – always have a game plan.
