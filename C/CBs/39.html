Raj Jansari posted this position on Facebook and had trouble understanding XG’s recommendation. I had the same problem when I studied it so let us try to make sense of the bot’s suggestion.

Before we come to that suggestion let us eliminate a few other plays. 13/6 leaves Black vulnerable to rolling 61 next turn so that cannot be right. 8/5, 8/5 eliminates the 61 problem but now Black will have more trouble clearing his mid-point. In certain variations he will want to keep his 8-pt so that he can play a roll of 5x by moving 13/x, 13/8, giving White a single shot. Moving 8/5, 8/4 eliminates those variations so again that cannot be right.

In positions like this XG’s strategy is to move inside the home board so as to give Black more time to roll a clearing double and never leave a shot. However, it is how you move within the home board that now becomes the focus of this problem.

Black will want to keep some fives to play later so playing 6/3, 6/2 is not the right idea. Equally well Black will not want to leave a hole in his home board on the 4-pt or 5-pt that will cause him to miss with one of those number later in the bear-off. This should eliminate 5/2, 5/1. 6/2, 4/1 is better but risks a later hole on the 4-pt. 5/1, 4/1 is very similar to 6/2, 4/1.

So, what does that leave us with? The answer is XG’s recommendation of 6/3, 5/1 – a somewhat strange looking play. 6/3, 5/1 leaves a five to play later, fills the current hole on the 1-pt (without overcrowding it) and leaves the same number of checkers on the 6-pt and 5-pt which XG seems to approve of.

I performed a long rollout of this position and 6/3, 5/1 comes out top by a long way. The next two plays, 5/2, 5/1 and 5/1, 4/1 are tied but with an equity difference 0.06 less than 6/3, 5/1 which implies a big error for any move other than the correct play.

Can we be sure that XG has got this one right. I believe so. This Is a relatively simple position to play (except of course getting the first move right!) and I would not expect a neural net program to make any mistakes from here. That means that we need to learn to think more about the ideas and analysis that I have gone through above. These ideas are perhaps more important than I would have thought, and I have certainly learnt something by analysing this position so my thanks to Raj for posting it in the first place.
