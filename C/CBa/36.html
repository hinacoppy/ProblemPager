Money Play. How should Red play 63?
pips Red=118 White=122
This is a difficult choice.

Over the board 8/2*, 6/3 was tried but it is very difficult to bring home the game from there with three stripped outfield points. There are two reasonable moves: a) 15/9*, 11/8 gaining in the race and sending back a second White checker and b) 11/5, 8/5 strengthening the home board and leaving only a single blot.

I would certainly have gone for (a) despite leaving two blots but the additional gammon losses from that approach are enough to just swing the decision to b) although it is very close. After that play Red is 13 pips up in the race and if not hit he will have a relatively easy job to bring home his other checkers.

I think that over the board not many would players would elect for 11/5, 8/5.
