Match Play. Red trails 4-5 to 7. How should Red play 61?
pips Red=105 White=97
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
With a live cube 8/2, 6/5 would be clear. Any play that splits the back checkers would lose too many gammons.

Here, however, gammons are irrelevant and so Red needs to make the play that wins that the most games. It then becomes a choice between 23/16 and 23/22, 8/2. 23/16 gives some duplication of fives and it gets the nod over 23/22, 8/2 which is rated an error (just) by XG.

The key lesson here is that when you are freed from the worry of gammons you must look for the play that wins the highest percentage of gammons. In this instance this is achieved by challenging Whiteâ€™s blots immediately while Red has the stronger home board.
