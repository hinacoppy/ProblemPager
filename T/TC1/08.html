Playing 8/2 here is a gargantuan blunder, turning a position in which you have a significant advantage into a position in which your opponent has a significant advantage.
But even if you realize that 23/17 is mandatory, the choice between 17/14 and 8/5 is a difficult technical decision.
It might seem natural to minimize shots with 17/14, duplicating White's threes to hit.
However, 8/5 cleans up a blot and somewhat duplicates White's sixes to hit and escape.
The balance is a delicate one and seemingly small changes can tip things the other way; see the variants below, for example.
