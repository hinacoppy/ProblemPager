Money Play. How should Red play 44?
pips Red=144 White=139
After bar/21 I think most people would find 9/5*/1* here. The question is the last 4.

After most sequences Red will be doubling next turn and White will be passing and Red will not have to cover the blot on his ace-point. Therefore 10/6 is an overplay and Red may as well protect against White's 55 joker by keeping his 10-pt. 20/16 is the correct play.

Perhaps surprisingly, XG rates 10/6 with the final 4 as very close to a blunder. Always remember to consider imminent cube action when making a play and particularly if you have rolled a joker, before which cube action seemed a long way off.
