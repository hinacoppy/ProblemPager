Money Play. How should Red play 64?
pips Red=119 White=181
The key to this position is understanding the required strategy.

White has a well-timed back game but is not yet ready to hit and contain a blot so he is unlikely to hit from Red’s 1-pt or 4-pt even if he gets the chance.

However, Red should not run from White’s bar-point with 18/8 as that might allow White to win by going forward. Red should position his checkers in his home board where he wants them. Given that at some point he will need to make his 2-pt he should do so now with 8/2, 6/2.

10/6, 8/2, triplicating ones, is also OK . 18/8 is a blunder.
