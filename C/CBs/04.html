Money Play. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=147 White=46
This is a case of ‘slowly, slowly, catchee monkey’.

Red must respect White’s three-point home board. 9/5* is just too bold, five blots are just too many and the gammon losses too expensive.

After 6/5(2)*, Red should play 13/12, 16/15, giving four builders for his 6-pt on his next roll. This pays off to 66 and 64 but Red must take some risks to build his board.
