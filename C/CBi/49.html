Money play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=121 White=155
The 10-pt is held by two of White's points so that is the point to clear rather than the 11-pt. The correct play is 11/8, 10/7(2), 6/3.

This play has the hidden advantage that if for instance White threw back-to-back awkward 6's (e.g. 63 followed by 62) he would have to give up Red's 4-pt.

Clearing the 11-pt with 11/8(3), 6/3 is very nearly a blunder!
