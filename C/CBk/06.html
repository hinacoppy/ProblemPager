Match Play. Red leads 5-4 to 7. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=55 White=77
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This position is a classic demonstration of the muddy thinking surrounding match play. Many would not double (and be automatically redoubled) because of the fear of losing the match.

The doubling window at this score is from 67% to 75%. The key question is whether Red’s winning chances fall within this window. The likelihood is yes because White will often get a single shot with a few extras and he will hit circa 30 – 35% of the time. The race is pretty much a lock so if not hit Red should win easily.

In fact, Red wins 69% of the time indicating double and take. Not doubling is a bad error. Red must know how to play rolls like 51 and 61 where the correct plats are 17/11 and 17/10 respectively. He should not hit 4/3* with the 1. He has lots of life after death if one checker is hit but not two.
