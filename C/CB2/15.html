Money Game. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=145 White=134
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one is quite surprising. Many players would select 24/20, 6/4 but that doesn't really improve Red's position that much.

Red would like to prime White and to do that he would like to make the 8-pt or the 3-pt. He also wants maximum outfield coverage in case White rolls a number such as 62 or 35. Note that slotting the back of the prime with 12/8, 6/4 is the wrong idea.

The way for Red to play to his plan is the surprising 20/14!! It is rare to give up the 5-pt but unless White rolls 33 or 11 he can't do a great deal of harm and 20/14 gives Red superb board coverage and real chances to improve his position next time.

We are so inured to maintaining the 5-pt anchor that few would even consider 20/14. It just goes to show that you cannot play backgammon on automatic but must consider each position on its merits
