Match Play. Red leads 6-5 to 7 (Crawford Game). How should Red play 65?
pips Red=96 White=86
For money, with the cube in any position, any ply other than 22/11 would be a blunder as leaping the partial prime is of paramount importance.

At 6-5 Crawford Red must be wary of losing a gammon and the match and therefore more caution must be exercised. 22/11 is now an error. 8/2, 7/2 is reasonable but leaves two blots so disaster could strike. XG rates it on a par with 22/11.

The correct play is 13/2. This play leaves a single blot in a place where Red doesn't mind getting hit and, importantly, retains the solid five-point prime.

This just goes to show how much care one must take at this particular score.
