Money Game. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=90 White=150
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one comes from the Battersea chouette. This is the sort of position where I used to take a roll but in recent months I have been applying Woolsey's Law much more rigorously and with a any sniff of gammon I am likely to double.

Two of us doubled, the other two members waited. We rolled 52, hit the checker on the 2-pt. White fanned (the other two players cashing their sure point) and we coasted to gammon. QED.

What's more XG supports us. Not doubling here is a double blunder and more. Red leads in the race, position and threat and it's extremely volatile. it just cries out for a double.

Of course you will lose some games from here and starting with something like 55 certainly doesn't help but you need to think ”what can go right?” rather than ‘what can go wrong?” Optimists make better backgammon players than pessimists!
