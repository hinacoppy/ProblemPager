For money and with the cube with White anything other than 8/5(2), 7/4(2)* would be a bad blunder. However, this is the Crawford game and all Red needs to do is win the game, gammons are irrelevant.

To that end now any play other 13/10(2), 11/8, 6/3(2) is a blunder. The resultant position is compact and likely to play very well for Red. As ever the match score is the driving force here.
