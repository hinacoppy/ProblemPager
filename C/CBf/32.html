Match Play. 0-0 to 7. How should Red play 62?
pips Red=100 White=72
Over the board 10/8, 10/4 was played, minimising shots for the next roll, but leaving future problems.

Making the 2-pt is crucial because if White misses whatever shot he is left Red wants as strong a board as possible so that he can attack White’s blots.

Surprisingly 15/9, 4/2 just edges out 10/1 in the rollouts but it is very close indeed. White doesn’t have much time to pick up the second blot and so he rarely closes out two checkers.

It turns out that keeping the 10-pt is worth the risk, not something I would have considered over the board.
