Money Play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=112 White=162
Against a back game you want to dismantle points, not create them.

Superficially, 13/10(2), 6/3(2) may look good but it leaves an unwieldy structure with stripped points everywhere. !3/7(2) creates a point that Red doesn’t want and will subsequently have to dismantle in front of two anchors. It is worse than a double blunder.

Correct is 13/10(2), 11/8(2). This creates flexibility and dismantles the awkward 11-pt. Any other move is at least a blunder. The concepts in this apparently simple position are extremely important – remember them.
