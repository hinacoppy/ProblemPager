Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=122 White=112
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
The position is volatile so Red should consider doubling. His five-point prime is strong, particularly with White one pip from the edge. On the down side it is easy to see Red escaping one checker and then having the other one closed out.

Even when that happens White still has to escape his own rear checker. On average it will take him five rolls to escape that checker if Red’s position doesn’t change.

How does this all add up? The take is trivial as White has many ways to win the game. The double is close but correct with Red’s gammons just making the difference between double and no double.
