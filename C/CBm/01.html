This is the sort of position that can cause lots of argument in a chouette because there are many possible moves that play to different game plans.

We can eliminate 14/8, 13/8 which creates a nice four-point prime but leaves Red with a split army. 13/8, 13/7 is not much better as it doesn’t really improve Red’s position.

The real choice is between the running play 22/11 and the more aggressive hitting play 14/3*. The race is close enough to make 22/11 a sensible option while the hitting play at least tries to take the initiative.

Neither play is ideal but sometimes you have to make do in backgammon – this position is a case in point.

In the rollout 14/3* just edges out 22/11 by a fraction but either move is acceptable.
