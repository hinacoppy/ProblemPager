Money Play. How should Red play 33?
pips Red=122 White=147
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This is all about tempo.

Red must enter with bar/22. After that he could play 13/10(3) but that gives White complete freedom to attack so Red must hit with 6/3*. Many players would then select 13/7 or 13/10(2) with the other two threes.

Both of these two moves would be blunders as is bar/22, 13/10(3). For once the ugly play, bar/22, 6/3(3)*, is correct. This leaves Red with just one blot to worry about and gives him (hopefully) the time to attack White's other two blots and perhaps win a gammon.

Many would discard the correct play because we learn not to give up the 6-pt and also not to bury checkers but here logic dictates that the ugly play is clearly the correct play.
