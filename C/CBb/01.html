Match Play. 3-5 to 7. How should Red play 52?
pips Red=142 White=120
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Position submitted by Leo Waters

This is very difficult because it is not clear what the best game plan is.

Blitzing with 13/11, 6/1* or 8/1* looks unlikely to succeed and the safe play, 13/6, does nothing to develop Red’s position. It also takes the pressure off the White blot on Red’s 10-pt.

Running a back checker when behind in the race and with a weaker home board is not a good idea. What is left?

Unlikely though it seems priming is the right idea. 8/3, 6/4 is the play and if hit Red can revert to a back game or a holding game. If not hit he has good chances to swiftly build a prime in front of White’s rear checker. It duplicates 3’s and keeps the pressure on White’s checker on the 10-pt.

Nearly everybody would get this wrong over the board.
