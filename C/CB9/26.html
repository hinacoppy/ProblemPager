Money Play. How should Red Play 21?
pips Red=153 White=156
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This one comes from a GridGammon match involving Paul Weaver (he was White).

Red played 24/23, 13/11 which I though was a reasonable try. Mentally I had played 24/23, 24/22 but I wasn’t sure so noted down the position

The rollout results surprised me. Clearly best according to XG is 11/9*, 24/23 exposing five blots! I think the idea is partially to slow Red down so White’s timing collapses and hopefully build a solid five-prime.

Even having seen the rollout results this is one of those rare positions where I still have my doubts and I would want to play it a few times with both rolls to get a feel for how it plays out which is all part of the learning curve. If I get time I will post any further analysis that I manage to do.
