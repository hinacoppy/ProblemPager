Money Play. How should Red play 22?
pips Red=92 White=112
Keeping the prime is the wrong plan here. After 8/2, 6/4 Red’s prime may well crack next time anyway.

Against a five-point board the loose hit is too loose. Therefore Red must play 3/1(2)* with two of the 2’s. He shouldn’t leave any extraneous blots lying around and so 8/4 is very clear for other two 2’s. Hopefully White will stay on the bar and give Red time to roll a six and, in a perfect world, pick up the White’s loose blot.

Any move other than 8/4, 3/1(2)* is an error or worse.
