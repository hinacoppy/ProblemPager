Money Play. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=129 White=157
Over the board I played 13/4* without much thought, seeking to take advantage of a strong home board and sniffing a gammon in the air. However, one should be wary of such hits when there is only direct cover next turn.

It turns out that 24/15 is the better play. It escapes a rear checker and neatly duplicates White's 3's. In fact with virtually all 3's White should anchor rather than hit on his next roll.

If Red does hit he should do so with 8/4*, 8/3, preserving his mid-point. This is still an error while 13/4* is technically a blunder despite some duplication of 1's.

The gain in gammons from hitting doesn't compensate for the additional losses Red suffers after hitting. It goes to show that you can't just play on general theory â€“ you have to examine the specifics of each position.
