This belongs to a class of position that XG gets wrong on evaluation but corrects itself on rollout after it looks deeper.

White wins nearly a third of the time from here. His problem is that over half his losses are gammons. This makes the position a pass but only by 0.03 (just an error).

Of course if White thinks Red will misplay the position (for example not hitting loose on his 5-pt when White has one checker there and one already on the bar) then White can take.

Note that Red is nowhere near strong enough to play on for a gammon from the start position. Not redoubling here is a double blunder.
