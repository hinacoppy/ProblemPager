2016 - Position 188

Money Play. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=125 White=78
In such situations it is normally correct to keep a solid five-point prime and so 9/4 or 9/8, 9/5 is indicated. Given that White will probably win with a redouble if he escapes it seems sensible to keep the extra builder with 9/4 for those occasions when he doesn’t escape although it is close because White may play on for a gammon after hitting.

If White had a weaker home board then 8/3* would be a viable alternative but, because of the two blots and White’s five-point board, 8/3* is just too loose because of the extra gammons lost.

9/4 wins the rollout by a very small margin.
