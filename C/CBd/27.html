Match Play. Red trails 4-5 to 7. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=137 White=117
It may surprise many that this position is a double (and a very clear take) for money, if only just. White’s prime is very fragile and numbers like 14 and 24 crack it immediately. Meanwhile Red’s timing is superior with those three spare checkers on his mid-point. This results in a high percentage of gammons for Red.

That last sentence will give you the clue for the correct match play action that is double and pass. Taking is a blunder. White must pass and play from 5-5.
