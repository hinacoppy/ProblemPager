Money Play. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=86 White=134
I had this position in a GridGammon match and played bar/24, 8/7(2), 4/3 on reflex but that was playing without thinking. It leaves a very inflexible position and some 5's next time will play very badly.

What I didn't see was bar/24, 8/5 which does leave a very flexible position where White will hit the blot on the 8-pt at his peril. A return hit by Red will likely spell ‘gammon'.

This is a case where I didn't ‘see' the play because I assumed I shouldn't leave a blot but in backgammon you must not discount plays because they don't seem to fit your model of the game. Given more time and study the play is very clear but in the heat of the moment over the board I missed it completely.
