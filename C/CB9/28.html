Money Play. How should Red play 32?
pips Red=44 White=39
3/off, 3/1 takes a checker off but leaves an ugly and inefficient structure with those three checkers piled on the 1-pt. The structure also leaves the danger of later shots.

Much better is 6/4, 6/3 which will keep a stronger board for longer. Red must also consider when he can redouble. If he plays 6/4, 6/3 and then takes another checker off while White fans he will have a strong redouble as he will very likely still have a five-point board.

That preservation of the home board for longer is the key to the position.
