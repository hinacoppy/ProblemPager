Double Match Point. How should Red play 41?
pips Red=162 White=119
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
One I managed to get wrong over the board because I made the money play.

Bar/21, 13/12* gives Red more gammons but here is he is only interested in winning the game and bar/21, 8/7 wins more games that bar/21, 13/12*. This latter play leaves four blots and things can quickly go wrong for Red.

Bar/21, 8/7 is the quiet play that creates a four-point prime and meanwhile White still has a blot on his mid-point to clear up before he can escape his rear checker (although he will run with rolls like 62 and 63). Red's strategy is then to prime and/or blitz White's rear checker.

The key here is to remember that it is DMP and checker plays at this score are frequently different from the money play.
