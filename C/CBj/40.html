Money Play. How should Red play 42?
pips Red=149 White=149
This is yet another advertisement for not conducting a war on two fronts. Any play that involves splitting the back checkers is at least a double blunder!

Red's plan should be to complete a five- or six-point prime on his side of the board and then worry about the rear checkers. The best play by a long way is 13/11, 13/9 with 13/9, 11/9 the only other play that isn't at least a blunder.

Lesson learnt!
