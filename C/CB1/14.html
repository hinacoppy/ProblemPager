Money Play. Should Red redouble? If redoubled, should White take?
pips Red=25 White=44
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
To solve this one you need to understand Effective Pip Counts. If you don't know anything about EPCs I suggest you download my monograph on Backgammon Races from the download section of this website.

Straight racing formula and more complex formulae such as the Thorpe Count and the Ward Count (plus Trice variation) all get this one wrong.

I am not going in to all the detail of the analysis here but suffice it to say that Red has a 7-roll position (for all intents and purposes) and therefore an EPC of 50. White has an actual pip count of 44 plus wastage of somewhere between 8 and nine pips giving him an EPC of 52.5.

Plug these numbers into Trice's formula and you get a borderline redouble and an easy take. I had a partner in the box as Red and all four of our opponents dropped this! Score one for the mathematicians and thanks to Walter Trice (RIP).
