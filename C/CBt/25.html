Money Play. How should Red play 53?
pips Red=148 White=77
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
Red cannot sensibly hold his two points in White’s board and should now look to win by going forwards.

The best way to do that is to move the two checkers on White’s 5-pt into the outfield to provide outer board coverage while retaining White’s 2-pt as a back-up plan.

The simple 20/17, 20/15 is best. 20/12 is second best but that gives White some potential counter-play against the blot left on White’s 5-pt.
