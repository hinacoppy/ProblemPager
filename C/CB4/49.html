Money Game. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=90 White=73
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
This basically a decision on whether to execute a trap play with 8/5, 8/4 or hold the position with 15/8.

In fact Red wins the same percentage of games with either play but wins 4% more gammons with the trap play (consider White's 51). White's board is just weak enough to execute the trap while the Red's checker on the 15-pt is far enough back to catch a fleeing White checker.

Give White a five-point home board and then the two plays have virtually identical equities.
