Match Play. Red trails 1-2 to 5. How should Red play 43?
pips Red=101 White=120
At virtually any other score or for money 13/10, 13/9 would be very clear.

However, 4-away vs. 3-away is a very special score and especially when the cube has been turned, as above.

A gammon wins the match for Red with perfect efficiency and that makes 9/2* the right play by quite some way. Now 13/10, 13/9 is an error!

The key point is to understand the influence of the match score on checker movement at this specific score and once learnt, to employ the tactic in future situations.
