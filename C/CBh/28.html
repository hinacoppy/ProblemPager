Money Play. How should Red play 11?
pips Red=153 White=137
The point of the position is to ensure that after bar/24 and the obvious 6/5 Red must keep the two points blocking White’s sixes on his side of the board.

10/9(2) would be a big error. Just take a look at how White’s sixes play after that move rather than 16/15(2).

16/15(2) is much better than 13/12(2) as the two checkers on the 15-pt then bear directly on the empty 9-pt.

Any move other than bar/24, 16/15(2), 6/5 is at the very least a blunder.
