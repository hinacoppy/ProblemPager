This is logical. Red has 20 numbers to pick up the second checker and 33, 44 and 66 are also strong. Those rolls don’t necessarily win but most of them are market-losing rolls.

From White’s perspective his six checkers off give him an easy take. He only wins 21% of the time from here but nearly half those wins are gammons and that makes the take quite straightforward.

Not redoubling is a blunder.
