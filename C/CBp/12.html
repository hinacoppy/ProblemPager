If White had his 5-pt Red would have to play safe with 10/8, 9/8.

However, the blot on White's 5-pt gives Red the opportunity to clear his bar-point with 7/6, 7/5 and create four builders for his 4-pt which is the point he would really like to make.

That gap on the 4-pt will give Red long-term problems so the risk of 7/6, 7/5 is worth the reward. Any move other than 7/6, 7/5 is at least an error. Over the board I played 10/7 and that is nearly a blunder as it just moves a builder for the 4-pt from one place to another.
