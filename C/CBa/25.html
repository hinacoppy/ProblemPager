The key here is to recognise that Red is not in a 1-3 back game – at least not yet.

His pip count deficit is not enough to play a true back game and trying to engineer it with moves like 13/11, 13/7 or 11/9, 11/5 is the wrong plan.

All of Red’s checkers in the outer boards are well placed and so the logical move is 24/16. This is an attempt to go forward while holding the 3-pt game in reserve. Of course Red may yet get forced into a back game but he should at least try to go forwards initially.
