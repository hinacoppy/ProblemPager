Money Play. Should Red double? If doubled, should White take?
pips Red=129 White=145
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
A classic 4-pt holding game and surprisingly enough the doubling decisions follow the norm for such positions.

The position is relatively non-volatile. Red's racing lead is not yet decisive and he may not escape his rear checker easily. On the plus side for Red he does have three of White's checkers behind a five-point prime.

Red is not yet quite good enough to double and White has a trivial take but of course if you think your opponent might pass then go ahead and double. Take White's spare checker from Red's 4-pt and put it on the 3-pt and then the position becomes double/take.
