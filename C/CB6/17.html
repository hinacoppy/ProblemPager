Money Play. How should Red play 54?
pips Red=124 White=109
Hopefully nobody found 9/4, 6/2 which is the equivalent of waving a white flag.

When I set the problem I assumed that my over the board play of 8/3, 7/3, making a broken prime would be the easy winner. Imagine my surprise when it dead-heated with the wide-open 9/5*, 6/1*!

This latter play seeks to build a counter-prime by force. It can backfire spectacularly but when it works it reaps a rich reward. The play wins 24% of the time which many will find a surprisingly high percentage.

The position is worthy of a longer analysis and commentary but for now just enjoy the result.
