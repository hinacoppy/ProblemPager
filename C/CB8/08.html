Match Play. Red leads 6-2 to 7 (Crawford Game). How should Red play 31?
pips Red=61 White=101
This is not easy because Red will have difficulty clearing the 15-pt whatever he does. The problem with 15/12, 3/2 is that it leaves two blots and too many shots against a five-point board.

Because of this 6/5*, 6/3 playing with just one blot is the correct play but only by a small margin precisely because Red will still have a lot of trouble clearing the 15-pt.

6/5*, 6/3 is only 0.0017 ahead of 15/12, 3/2 in the rollout.
