Match Play. Red leads White 1-0 to 5. Should Red double? If doubled should White take?
pips Red=144 White=155
(CorrectAnswer= 0/0, ErrorScore= 0)
For money this is a perfectly correct double and an equally correct take. After all Red does have two checkers on the bar and White's ace-point anchor keeps him in the game to the end. The volatility of the position means that Red must double now.

At the match score losing a gammon is ruinous for White as he loses the match immediately. This converts a money take into a match score pass, albeit not by that much.

When a gammon for my opponent can give him the match or take him to Crawford I always spend a little more time on my decision and err on the side of caution.
