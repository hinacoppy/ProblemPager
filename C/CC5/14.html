This is really a problem about connectivity. The 15-pt should be kept for communication so 15/3(2) would be a bad error.

22/16(2) seems clear. The question then is how to play the other two sixes.

22/10(2) is the second-best play and not an error and 22/16(2), 9/3(2) is also OK. The most flexible and therefore the best play is 22/16(2), 21/9. This also optimises the communication between the Red checkers.
