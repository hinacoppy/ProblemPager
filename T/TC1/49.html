After the obvious 8/7*(2), Black has to weigh various priorities. He is way behind in the pip count, so he wants to block White's anchor if he can, and perhaps make some home-board points to cause trouble for White's straggler. His back checkers are getting primed, so he would like to try to escape them, but without giving White too many opportunities to attack with her 12 checkers in the zone.
The eXtreme Gammon rollout favors 11/10(2), which gives up the six-away point but keeps an eye on the 4pt in case White enters there.
Black's outfield checkers are not in any danger of getting hit, so keeping them on separate points gives him more ways to make another outfield blocking point next roll.
But note that Black's balancing of priorities would be much trickier if his 4pt were already made (see Variant position below).
