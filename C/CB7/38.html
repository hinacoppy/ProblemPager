The follow-up to Position 37.

This is a simple lesson in not breaking your home board to maintain a defensive position. Over the board Red played 6/2 but that is a blunder as is any other play that breaks the board.

Red wants a hit to be a win and once his perfect board is compromised that may not be the case. The simple 21/20, 21/18 is correct, keeping the rear checker on the 23-pt for maximum contact. Any other play is at least a blunder.
